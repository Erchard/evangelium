# Logion 7 Evidence Note

Status: working evidence note v0.1.

## Decision

`UNCERTAIN`

## Candidate Text

English:

> Blessed is the lion that a human eats, so that the lion becomes human. Woe to the human whom a lion eats, so that the lion becomes human.

Ukrainian:

> Блаженний лев, якого з'їсть людина, і лев стане людиною. Горе людині, яку з'їсть лев, і лев стане людиною.

This candidate is not yet included in the main reconstructed text.

## Coptic Witness

Source: `02_Sources_And_Manuscripts/primary_texts/coptic/logia-001-007-coptic-working.md`.

The Coptic witness preserves the saying complete.

## Greek Witness

Source: `02_Sources_And_Manuscripts/primary_texts/greek_poxy/poxy654-transcription-working.md` and `02_Sources_And_Manuscripts/primary_texts/greek_poxy/dclp-poxy654-62840.xml`.

P.Oxy. 654, lines 40-42, is mostly reconstructed. Line 42 has an important variant between a woe-formula and an anathema-formula.

## Parallels

No direct synoptic parallel has been entered. The unit should be tested against wider ancient symbolism, including transformation, consumption, animal imagery, and beatitude/woe forms.

## Rationale

Reasons for caution:

- The saying is symbolically dense and has no direct synoptic control at this stage.
- The Greek witness is mostly reconstructed.
- The interpretive range is wide, so historical placement would be premature.

Reasons the unit remains important:

- It is a strong test case for non-synoptic Thomas material.
- It may preserve an independent symbolic saying.
- Its beatitude/woe shape may connect it to broader early wisdom traditions even without a direct gospel parallel.

## Probability Assessment

| Period | Probability | Reason |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Before 60 CE | 10% | Possible only if the symbolic saying has an early independent oral origin. |
| 60-90 CE | 20% | Possible as early non-synoptic wisdom material, but evidence is thin. |
| 90-130 CE | 45% | Best fits a developed Thomasine symbolic environment. |
| After 130 CE | 25% | Possible for final shaping, though the Greek witness keeps the unit from being treated as merely late Coptic. |

## Remaining Work

- Search for ancient parallels to lion/human consumption symbolism.
- Study beatitude and woe formulas in non-synoptic material.
- Recheck the Greek variants before any public inclusion decision.
