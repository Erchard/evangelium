# Logion 27 Evidence Note

Status: evidence note v0.1.

## Decision

`UNCERTAIN`

Logion 27 should not be added to the Ukrainian reader text at this stage.

## Working Core

Extant form:

> If you do not fast from the world, you will not find the kingdom; if you do not sabbatize the Sabbath, you will not see the Father.

No shorter early core can currently be isolated with confidence.

## Main Reason

Logion 27 is textually important but reconstructionally unstable.

It has a strong manuscript profile: complete Coptic Thomas, a strong Greek witness in P.Oxy. 1, and an additional early control in P.Oxy. 5575 for material close to Thomas 27. This confirms that the saying or a closely related form circulated in Greek sayings contexts.

However, the saying's main content is a symbolic reinterpretation of ritual practice. The phrase "fast from the world" has no direct synoptic parallel, and "sabbatize the Sabbath" is not the form of Sabbath reinterpretation found in Mark 2:23-28, Matthew 12:1-8, or Luke 6:1-5. The extant saying therefore fits the Thomas ritual-ethics cluster better than it fits a securely recoverable earliest Jesus-saying layer.

## Greek Witness: P.Oxy. 1

P.Oxy. 1 preserves the saying in Greek:

> Ἰησοῦς· ἐὰν μὴ νηστεύσητε τὸν κόσμον οὐ μὴ εὕρητε τὴν βασιλείαν τοῦ θεοῦ· καὶ ἐὰν μὴ σαββατίσητε τὸ σάββατον οὐκ ὄψεσθε τὸν πατέρα.

This witness is much stronger than the partial Greek witness for Logion 26 because it preserves the whole saying. It confirms that Thomas 27 is not merely a late Coptic formulation.

## P.Oxy. 5575

P.Oxy. 5575 was published in 2023 in *The Oxyrhynchus Papyri* vol. 87. NASSCAL summarizes it as a second-century fragment whose recto includes material parallel to Matthew 6:25 / Luke 12:22, then material found in Thomas 27, then material parallel to Matthew 6:26 / Luke 12:24. Gospels.net provides a public-domain English translation based on the edition.

This matters because it shows Thomas-27-like material in an early Greek sayings environment outside the direct P.Oxy. 1 / Coptic Thomas line. But it also cautions against overclaiming: the fragment is mixed and fragmentary, so it may show adaptation, harmonization, or free circulation rather than a stable earliest gospel text.

## Synoptic Comparison

Detailed table: `04_Synoptic_Parallels/logion-027-fasting-sabbath.md`.

Fasting controls:

- Matthew 6:16-18 regulates fasting without public display.
- Mark 2:18-20, Matthew 9:14-15, and Luke 5:33-35 connect fasting with the presence or absence of the bridegroom.

Sabbath controls:

- Mark 2:23-28, Matthew 12:1-8, and Luke 6:1-5 reinterpret Sabbath through human need and the authority of the Son of Man.
- Luke's healing-on-Sabbath materials further develop the question around doing good and saving life.

These are important thematic controls, but none directly parallels the two formulas that define Thomas 27.

## Reconstruction Implication

Move Logion 27 from `DEFER` to `UNCERTAIN`: the separate fasting/Sabbath study has now been done.

Do not add it to the main Ukrainian reader text. Use it in the evidence dossier as a key example of a well-attested saying that should still be withheld from the earliest-layer reconstruction because attestation alone does not prove primitive status.
