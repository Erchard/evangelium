## Main Evidence

Thomas 93 preserves the holy/dogs and pearls/swine unit known from Matthew 7:6. The Coptic text includes the Greek loanword for pearls, which supports a plausible Greek Vorlage but does not constitute an extant Greek Thomas witness.

The saying is short, vivid, and proverb-like. At the same time, it can easily function as a secrecy saying about restricting revelation to insiders, a theme that fits Thomas's collectional ethos.

## Early-Core Assessment

Possible early core:

> Do not give what is holy to dogs; do not cast pearls before swine.

The core may be an early proverb adapted into Jesus tradition, but its precise Thomas form is not securely independent.

## Secondary-Layer Indicators

Matthew is the only close canonical control. Thomas's wording about throwing into dung and trampling may reflect independent imagery, textual fluidity, or secondary elaboration. The logion's insider/outsider function could also be intensified by Thomasine secrecy theology.

## Limits

No extant Greek Thomas witness is loaded. One-source Matthean control limits confidence. The Greek loanword `margarites` supports a Greek background but not a manuscript witness.
