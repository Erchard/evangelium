## Main Evidence

Thomas 78 shares the wilderness, reed, and soft-clothing complex known from Matthew and Luke in the John the Baptist tradition. Thomas does not explicitly name John and ends with a truth/knowledge contrast about rulers and fine clothing.

## Early-Core Assessment

Possible early core:

> Why did you go out into the wilderness? To see a reed shaken by the wind? To see a person in soft clothing?

This is strongly controlled by Matthew and Luke.

## Secondary-Layer Indicators

The final statement about those in soft clothing not knowing truth may be Thomasine interpretation or secondary moralization. It should not be included without marker.

## Limits

No extant Greek Thomas witness is loaded. Thomas may preserve an independent form, but it may also extract and reframe the John tradition from synoptic-like material.
