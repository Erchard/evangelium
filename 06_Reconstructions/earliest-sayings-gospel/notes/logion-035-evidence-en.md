# Logion 35 Evidence Note

Status: evidence note v0.1.

## Decision

`INCLUDE_WITH_MARKER`

## Working Core

> No one can enter the strong man's house and plunder it unless he first binds the strong man.

## Main Reason

Logion 35 is complete in Coptic and has strong synoptic controls in Mark 3:27 and Matthew 12:29. Luke 11:21-22 preserves a related but more developed form about the armed strong man and a stronger attacker.

Thomas is especially close to the Mark/Matthew structure: entering the strong man's house, binding, and then plundering or taking from the house. Thomas adds or preserves the detail of binding the strong man's hands.

No loaded Oxyrhynchus Greek Thomas witness preserves Logion 35. However, a Greek form is historically very likely because the saying is securely attested in Greek synoptic tradition. Any Greek form in the reconstruction should be labeled `Greek retroversion, hypothetical`.

## Reconstruction Judgment

The strong-man core is strong enough for marked inclusion. The marker remains necessary because Thomas 35 is Coptic-only in the loaded project sources and because the saying's original rhetorical context remains unresolved.

## Reconstruction Implication

Add Logion 35 to the Ukrainian reader with a marker.
