# Logion 11 Evidence Note

Status: working evidence note v0.1.

## Decision

`UNCERTAIN`

Do not add Logion 11 to the main reconstructed reader text yet.

## Coptic Witness

Source: `02_Sources_And_Manuscripts/primary_texts/coptic/linssen-thomas-coptic-unicode-working.txt`.

Thomas 11 is complete in the Coptic witness. It is compositionally dense and combines several motifs:

- this heaven and the heaven above it passing away;
- dead not living and living not dying;
- eating what is dead and making it living;
- being in the light;
- one becoming two.

## Greek Witness

No loaded Greek Oxyrhynchus witness preserves Thomas 11. Any Greek form must be labeled `Greek retroversion, hypothetical`.

## Subunit Analysis

### A. Heaven Passing

Possible English rendering:

> This heaven will pass away, and the one above it will pass away.

This has partial control in Mark 13:31, Matthew 24:35, and Luke 21:33:

> Heaven and earth will pass away, but my words will not pass away.

The control is important but not sufficient for reader inclusion. Thomas has "this heaven and the one above it," not "heaven and earth," and it lacks the stabilizing contrast about Jesus' words.

### B. Dead and Living

Thomas says that the dead are not living and the living will not die. This belongs near Thomas's immortality language in Logia 1, 18, 19, and 111, but it is not securely separable as an earliest saying.

### C. Eating the Dead

The saying about eating what is dead and making it living has no strong synoptic control currently entered. It may reflect Thomasine or wider ascetic-symbolic anthropology.

### D. Light

The light question belongs near Thomas's internal light language, especially Logia 24, 50, and 61. This is not yet strong enough for reader inclusion.

### E. One / Two

The one/two material belongs to the Thomas unity cluster also seen in Logia 22, 23, 30, and 106. The project already treats this cluster as a warning marker for possible Thomasine redaction unless independently supported.

## Probability Assessment

| Period | Probability | Reason |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Before 60 CE | 10% | A heaven-passing motif could have early roots, but the full unit is too composite. |
| 60-90 CE | 25% | The heaven-passing motif overlaps with synoptic apocalyptic tradition. |
| 90-130 CE | 45% | The extant Thomas form fits Thomasine symbolic and anthropological development. |
| After 130 CE | 20% | Possible for final collection shaping or Coptic-preserved form. |

## Reconstruction Implication

Do not add Logion 11 to `reconstructed-gospel-uk.md`.

Keep the full logion in the apparatus as `UNCERTAIN`. The heaven-passing subunit is the strongest part, but it is not yet strong enough to print as a reconstructed core.
