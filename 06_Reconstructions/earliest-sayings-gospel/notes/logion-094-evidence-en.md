## Main Evidence

Thomas 94 is a very short seek/find and knock/open saying. Matthew and Luke preserve the fuller ask/seek/knock triad. Thomas lacks the asking element and gives a compact two-part form.

The saying is strongly aligned with a recurring Thomas motif: seeking until finding. Because Logion 2 is already included with a marker, Logion 94 must be assessed as either a second independent witness to the seeking tradition or a redundant collectional doublet.

## Early-Core Assessment

Possible early core:

> The one who seeks will find; to the one who knocks, it will be opened.

This core is short, aphoristic, and strongly controlled by Matthew/Luke, but it may duplicate material already represented by Logion 2.

## Secondary-Layer Indicators

The absence of "ask" may be primitive abbreviation or Thomasine selection. The logion could have been added because it resonates with the collection's programmatic search motif. Logion 92 also shows that seek/find material can appear in expanded dialogue form in Thomas.

## Limits

No extant Greek Thomas witness is loaded. The saying's strength as early tradition must be balanced against duplication inside the reconstructed reader. Inclusion would require deciding whether the clean text should preserve both Logion 2 and this shorter doublet.
