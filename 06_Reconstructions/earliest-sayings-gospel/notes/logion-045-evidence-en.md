## Main Evidence

Logion 45 contains at least two traditional units:

1. grapes/thorns and figs/thistles;
2. good person / evil person bringing forth from the treasure of the heart.

Both units have strong synoptic controls. Luke 6:43-45 is especially important because it combines tree/fruit and heart/speech material in one complex.

## Early-Core Assessment

Possible early cores:

> Grapes are not gathered from thorns, nor figs from thistles.

and:

> A good person brings forth good from the heart's treasure; an evil person brings forth evil.

Both are plausible wisdom-style sayings. The heart/speech line may be slightly more developed but remains strongly controlled.

## Secondary-Layer Indicators

The full Thomas form is composite. The repeated treasure/heart/speech material may represent conflation of related sayings. It should not be included as one unmarked unit.

## Limits

No loaded extant Greek Thomas witness. The note still needs a line-by-line comparison with Matthew and Luke.
