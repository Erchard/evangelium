## Main Evidence

Thomas 95 joins two related ethical motifs: do not lend at interest, and give to someone from whom repayment is not expected. Luke 6 controls the radical lending-without-return motif. Jewish legal and wisdom traditions control the anti-usury motif.

This logion is ethically strong and relatively unmythological. It may preserve a practical economic saying from a radical poverty/generosity stream.

## Early-Core Assessment

Possible early core:

> Do not lend at interest; give where you do not expect repayment.

This could be early ethical teaching, but the exact combination of anti-usury and non-repayment must be treated cautiously.

## Secondary-Layer Indicators

The opening is damaged in the Coptic witness. The saying may combine Jewish anti-interest law with Lukan-style radical generosity. It may therefore be a secondary harmonization of related ethical traditions rather than a single early saying.

## Limits

No extant Greek Thomas witness is loaded. Luke is the closest canonical control for the non-repayment element, while the anti-usury element is broader Jewish ethical background. The logion needs careful appendix treatment even if not included.
