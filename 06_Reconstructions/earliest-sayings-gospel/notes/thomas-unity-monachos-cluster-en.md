# Thomas Unity / Monachos Cluster

Status: cluster evidence note v0.1.

## Purpose

This note tests whether Thomas unity language - "become one," "make the two one," "stand as one," and `monachos` / solitary language - should be treated as a recoverable early sayings-gospel layer or as a Thomasine theological/redactional marker.

The immediate reason for this check is Logion 22. Its children-and-kingdom core is promising, but its unity-of-opposites expansion must be evaluated against the wider Thomas pattern before it can be included in the reconstruction.

## Main Decision

Unity / monachos language should be treated as a Thomasine theological marker unless it is supported by independent controls.

This does not mean that every unity saying is late. It means that repetition inside Thomas is not enough to prove earliest recoverability. For reconstruction purposes:

- keep externally supported early cores;
- mark or exclude attached unity expansions;
- do not add unity / monachos units to the Ukrainian reader text without separate evidence.

## Evidence Pattern

The cluster is internally coherent. It repeatedly links salvation, entry, election, or power with becoming one, standing as one, becoming solitary, or overcoming two-ness.

That coherence is important for understanding Thomas, but it cuts both ways. A repeated motif across the collection may preserve traditional material, yet it may also reveal the collection's own theological profile. Because the project aims to reconstruct the earliest recoverable gospel layer, internal repetition cannot be used as a shortcut to inclusion.

## External Controls

Open commentary controls identify the same pattern. Early Christian Writings / GospelThomas.com notes the importance of the solitary / `monachos` motif across sayings such as 11, 16, 22, 23, 49, 75, and 106. Its Saying 22 page also gathers parallels for the "two become one" complex, including 2 Clement 12, the Gospel of the Egyptians tradition, Galatians 3:28, and related materials.

These controls show that the motif complex circulated in early Christian and non-canonical settings. They do not prove that the full Thomas forms belong to the earliest recoverable Jesus tradition.

## Logion 22 Result

Logion 22 is now split by layer.

Included marked core:

> Little children are like those who enter the kingdom.

Decision: children/kingdom core `INCLUDE_WITH_MARKER`; full logion `UNCERTAIN`.

The unity expansion remains in the apparatus. The cluster check strengthens caution: the expansion matches a broad Thomasine pattern and should not be folded into the earliest reconstruction merely because it appears in several Thomas logia.

## Logion 16 Result

Logion 16 is now split by layer.

Included marked core:

> I did not come to bring peace, but division; a household will be divided.

Decision: division/household core `INCLUDE_WITH_MARKER`; full logion `UNCERTAIN`.

The fire / sword / war sequence and the final `monachos` closure remain in the apparatus. The `monachos` ending has no independent synoptic support and should not be included in the reader.

## Logion 23 Result

Logion 23 moves to `UNCERTAIN`.

Possible core:

> I shall choose one from a thousand and two from ten thousand, and they will stand as one.

Reason for caution: the logion is short, but it has no loaded Greek Thomas witness and no strong synoptic control. Its final "stand as one" belongs directly to the unity / elect / monachos pattern. It may preserve community identity language rather than an early independent Jesus saying.

## Project Rule

For future decisions:

1. If a logion has a strong external core plus unity language, evaluate the core separately from the unity layer.
2. If a logion is mainly unity / monachos language, keep it out of the reader text until it has independent support.
3. Use unity / monachos language as a warning marker for possible Thomasine redaction, not as automatic exclusion.

## Immediate Reconstruction Impact
