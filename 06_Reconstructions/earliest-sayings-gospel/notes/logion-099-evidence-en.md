## Main Evidence

Thomas 99 preserves the true-family saying: Jesus' real family consists of those who do the will of his Father. The core is widely controlled by Mark, Matthew, and Luke. Matthew is closest to Thomas's "will of my Father" wording, while Luke has a related "hear and do the word of God" form.

Thomas adds a kingdom-of-the-Father ending: those who do the Father's will will enter the kingdom. This ending is coherent within Thomas, but it is not necessary for the traditional family-redefinition core.

## Early-Core Assessment

Possible early core:

> Those who do the will of God are my brother and my mother.

The arrival of mother/brothers outside may be traditional narrative frame rather than independent saying core.

## Secondary-Layer Indicators

The kingdom-of-the-Father ending likely reflects Thomasine or collectional framing. The explicit "Father" wording may be old or Matthean-shaped; this must remain marked.

## Limits

No extant Greek Thomas witness is loaded. The saying has strong synoptic control, but Thomas may preserve a decontextualized gospel scene rather than an independent pre-synoptic form.
