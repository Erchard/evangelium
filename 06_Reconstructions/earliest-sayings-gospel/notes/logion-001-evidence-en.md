# Logion 1 Evidence Note

Status: working evidence note v0.1.

## Decision

`INCLUDE_WITH_MARKER`

## Reconstructed Function

Logion 1 is retained as a collection frame, not as an independently secure historical saying.

English:

> Whoever finds the interpretation of these words will not taste death.

Ukrainian:

> Той, хто знайде тлумачення цих слів, не скуштує смерті.

## Marker

This unit should be marked as a framing saying. It may preserve an early understanding of the sayings collection, but it probably belongs to the formation and reception of the collection rather than to the earliest recoverable Jesus tradition itself.

## Coptic Witness

Source: `02_Sources_And_Manuscripts/primary_texts/coptic/logia-001-007-coptic-working.md`.

The Coptic text preserves the logion complete in NHC II,2.

## Greek Witness

Source: `02_Sources_And_Manuscripts/primary_texts/greek_poxy/poxy654-transcription-working.md` and `02_Sources_And_Manuscripts/primary_texts/greek_poxy/dclp-poxy654-62840.xml`.

P.Oxy. 654, lines 3-5, preserves the transition from the prologue into Logion 1, but most of the saying is supplied in lacunae.

## Parallels

There is no direct synoptic parallel entered at this stage. John 8:51 is a thematic control for the phrase "not taste death," but it is not being used as an argument for synoptic dependence.

## Rationale

Reasons for inclusion:

- It is integral to both the Coptic witness and the Greek P.Oxy. 654 textual frame.
- It explains how the collection asks to be read: as words requiring interpretation.
- It is necessary for a transparent public edition of the reconstructed sayings collection.

Reasons for marking:

- The unit is a hermeneutical frame.
- The Greek evidence for the core wording is mostly reconstructed.
- The promise not to taste death may reflect collection-level theology more than the earliest layer of sayings.

## Probability Assessment

| Period | Probability | Reason |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Before 60 CE | 10% | Possible if the frame preserves early oral authority, but this is not the simplest explanation. |
| 60-90 CE | 25% | Plausible during an early phase of sayings collection formation. |
| 90-130 CE | 45% | Best fits a developed collection frame known already in Greek before the Coptic manuscript. |
| After 130 CE | 20% | Possible for later redaction, though P.Oxy. 654 prevents treating it as merely late Coptic growth. |
