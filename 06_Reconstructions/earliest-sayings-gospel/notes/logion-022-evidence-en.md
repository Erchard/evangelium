# Logion 22 Evidence Note

Status: evidence note v0.2.

## Decision

`INCLUDE_WITH_MARKER` for the children/kingdom core.

The full Logion 22 remains `UNCERTAIN`.

## Working Early Core

Possible early core:

> Little children are like those who enter the kingdom.

Alternative reconstructed control form:

> Whoever receives the kingdom like a little child enters it.

The first form is closer to the extant Coptic Thomas wording. The second form is closer to the Mark/Luke synoptic control. Since no Greek Thomas 22 witness is available in the loaded Oxyrhynchus material, neither form should be treated as final.

## Main Reason

Logion 22 is probably layered. The children-and-kingdom motif is strongly supported by synoptic parallels, but the extant Thomas logion expands that motif into a long unity-of-opposites sequence. That sequence is important for understanding Thomas, but it is not securely recoverable as the earliest layer of the gospel tradition.

## Layer Analysis

### Layer A: Children and Kingdom

This layer is compact and well attested as a traditional motif. Mark 10:14-15 and Luke 18:16-17 preserve a close form: children are not to be hindered, the kingdom belongs to such as these, and one must receive the kingdom like a child. Matthew 19:14 preserves the kingdom-belongs-to-such motif, while Matthew 18:3 turns the motif into a command to become like children.

Thomas 22 has a positive aphoristic form: little nursing children are like those who enter the kingdom. This is not identical to any synoptic wording, but it belongs to the same tradition field.

### Layer B: Disciples' Question

The disciples ask whether becoming little ones will allow them to enter the kingdom. This question may be a bridge generated from the initial aphorism. It turns the saying into a dialogue and creates space for the longer interpretive answer.

This bridge is not necessarily late by itself, but it is less secure than the initial children-and-kingdom core.

### Layer C: Unity of Opposites

The answer develops a long sequence:

- make the two one;
- inside as outside and outside as inside;
- upper as lower;
- male and female as one;
- eye/hand/foot/image replacement.

This layer is highly important for Thomasine theology. It also has known parallels in other early Christian and non-canonical materials. Early Christian Writings notes 2 Clement 12, the Gospel of the Egyptians, Galatians 3:28, and related unity traditions as controls for this complex.

However, this is exactly why the layer must be treated cautiously. The broader circulation of the motif complex shows that it was available for interpretive expansion; it does not prove that the full Thomas form belongs to the earliest recoverable gospel layer.
