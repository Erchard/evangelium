# Logion 39 Evidence Note

Status: evidence note v0.1.

## Decision

`INCLUDE_WITH_MARKER`

## Working Units

Unit A:

> The scribes and Pharisees took the key(s) of knowledge; they did not enter, and they did not allow those entering to enter.

Unit B:

> Be wise as serpents and pure as doves.

## Main Reason

Logion 39 is a composite but important unit. It has:

- complete Coptic Thomas;
- Greek P.Oxy. 655 witness, though heavily restored;
- a strong Luke 11:52 control for the key of knowledge;
- a strong Matthew 23:13 control for not entering and blocking those who enter;
- a strong Matthew 10:16 control for the serpent/dove saying.

The two parts should not be treated as one secure primitive composition. They are better handled as two marked cores joined in the transmitted Thomas form.

## Reconstruction Judgment

Unit A is promising because Luke 11:52 is unusually close: key of knowledge, not entering, and obstructing others. Matthew 23:13 confirms the same polemical structure in another form. The Thomas wording "knowledge" should not be dismissed as late merely because it can sound gnostic in Coptic; the Greek `γνώσεως` is directly controlled by Luke.

Unit B is promising because Matthew 10:16 provides close verbal control. Its attachment to Unit A in Thomas may be secondary, but the short saying itself is strong enough to print with a marker.

## Reconstruction Implication

Add Logion 39 to the Ukrainian reader as split marked units. Do not print it as an unmarked single composition.
