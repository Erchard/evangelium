## Main Evidence

Thomas 64 shares the core banquet pattern:

- a host prepares a meal;
- invited guests refuse;
- excuses involve property, trade, marriage, or obligations;
- others are invited instead.

Luke is the closest synoptic control. Matthew's wedding-banquet form is more allegorically developed.

## Early-Core Assessment

Possible early core:

> A host invited guests to a meal; those invited refused because of their affairs, so others were brought in.

This core is plausible as an early parable about invitation, refusal, and reversal.

## Secondary-Layer Indicators

Thomas's closing line excluding merchants and traders from the Father's place may be a secondary application. It should not be included with the parable core unless independently justified.
