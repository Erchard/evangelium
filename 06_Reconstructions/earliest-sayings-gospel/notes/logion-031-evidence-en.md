# Logion 31 Evidence Note

Status: evidence note v0.1.

## Decision

`INCLUDE_WITH_MARKER`

## Working Core

> A prophet is not accepted in his own homeland.

Marked paired expansion:

> Nor does a physician heal those who know him.

## Main Reason

Logion 31 has a strong evidence profile:

- complete Coptic Thomas;
- strong Greek P.Oxy. 1 witness;
- close synoptic controls in Mark 6:4, Matthew 13:57, and Luke 4:24.

The prophet-in-homeland saying is a short, memorable, widely attested proverbial unit.

The physician clause is also preserved in both Coptic and P.Oxy. 1, but its external support is weaker. Luke 4:23 has a related physician proverb in the same Nazareth context, yet it is not the same saying. Therefore the physician clause should be retained only with a marker.

## Reconstruction Implication

Add Logion 31 to the Ukrainian reader with a marker. Do not treat the physician clause as equally secure as the prophet core.
