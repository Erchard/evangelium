# Logion 25 Evidence Note

Status: evidence note v0.1.

## Decision

`INCLUDE_WITH_MARKER`

Logion 25 may be added to the Ukrainian reader text with a visible marker.

## Working Early Core

Possible early core:

> Love your neighbor / brother as yourself.

Thomas form:

> Love your brother as your soul; guard him like the apple of your eye.

## Main Reason

Logion 25 is short, ethical, and strongly rooted in Jewish and synoptic love-command tradition. The core has strong controls in Leviticus 19:18, Mark 12:31, Matthew 22:39, and Luke 10:27.

The saying still needs a marker because Thomas has "brother" rather than the broader "neighbor," and because no loaded Greek Thomas 25 witness is available. The "apple of the eye" clause is biblically plausible, but it is not part of the synoptic love-command form.

## Layer Analysis

### Layer A: Love Command

The strongest layer is the love command. Thomas has "brother" and "soul"; the synoptic controls have "neighbor" and "yourself." The Thomas form may reflect a Semitic expression where "soul" functions close to "self."

This layer is strong enough for marked inclusion.

### Layer B: Guard as the Apple of the Eye

The second clause has biblical and wisdom controls. It resembles language in Deuteronomy 32:10, Psalm 17:8, and Proverbs 7:2. It extends the love command into a protection/watchfulness image.

This clause may be early Jewish wisdom language, but it is not directly supported by the synoptic love-command parallels. It should therefore remain marked.
