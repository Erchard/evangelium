## Main Evidence

Logion 65 preserves the wicked-tenants pattern:

- owner plants or has a vineyard;
- tenants receive it;
- servants are sent;
- violence occurs;
- son/heir is sent;
- son is killed.

Logion 66 gives the rejected-stone saying, which in the synoptic tradition follows the tenants parable as scriptural interpretation.

## Early-Core Assessment

Possible early parable core:

> A vineyard owner sent servants, then his son, to tenants; the tenants killed the son.

Possible early scriptural saying:

> The stone rejected by the builders became the cornerstone.

The rejected-stone saying is scriptural rather than purely aphoristic, so its use may reflect interpretive development.

## Secondary-Layer Indicators

The tenants parable is highly allegorizable. The son/heir element and rejected-stone application may reflect post-Easter interpretation in some forms. Thomas's shorter shape may be earlier, but this cannot be assumed.

## Limits

This cluster needs careful literary-dependence work. It is not safe to include before comparing Mark, Matthew, Luke, Psalm 118, and Thomas line by line.
