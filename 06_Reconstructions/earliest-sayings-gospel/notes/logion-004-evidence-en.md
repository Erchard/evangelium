# Logion 4 Evidence Note

Status: working evidence note v0.1.

## Decision

`INCLUDE_WITH_MARKER`

## Reconstructed Early Core

English:

> Many who are first will become last, and the last first.

Ukrainian:

> Багато перших стануть останніми, і останні першими.

## Marked Expansion

English:

> An old person will not hesitate to ask a seven-day-old child about the place of life, and that person will live. They will become one.

Ukrainian:

> Стара людина не вагатиметься запитати семиденного малого про місце життя, і вона житиме. Вони стануть одним.

## Coptic Witness

Source: `02_Sources_And_Manuscripts/primary_texts/coptic/logia-001-007-coptic-working.md`.

The Coptic witness preserves the full saying, joining the seven-day child image with the first/last formula and the final "become one" motif.

## Greek Witness

Source: `02_Sources_And_Manuscripts/primary_texts/greek_poxy/poxy654-transcription-working.md` and `02_Sources_And_Manuscripts/primary_texts/greek_poxy/dclp-poxy654-62840.xml`.

P.Oxy. 654, lines 22-27, is heavily reconstructed but confirms the general structure of the saying. Line 26 has an important variant: "come to one" or, in Fitzmyer's reconstruction, "have eternal life."

## Synoptic Parallels

The first/last formula has synoptic parallels in Mark 10:31, Matthew 19:30; 20:16, and Luke 13:30. The seven-day child and place-of-life image has no direct synoptic equivalent entered at this stage.

Detailed synoptic table: `04_Synoptic_Parallels/logion-004-first-last.md`.

The new comparison confirms a strong formulaic parallel for the first/last core, especially with Mark 10:31 and Matthew 19:30. It does not support the seven-day child, the place of life, or the become-one ending as synoptic material.

## Rationale

Reasons for inclusion:

- The first/last saying is widely attested in early Jesus tradition.
- The concise reversal formula is suitable for an early sayings layer.
- P.Oxy. 654 confirms that a version of Logion 4 circulated in Greek.

Reasons for marking the expansion:

- The seven-day child image and "become one" ending are more distinctively Thomasine.
- The Greek witness is lacunose.
- The final wording is textually unstable.

## Probability Assessment

| Period | Probability | Reason |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Before 60 CE | 20% | The first/last reversal could plausibly be early. |
| 60-90 CE | 30% | Fits the period in which reversal sayings circulated in multiple streams. |
| 90-130 CE | 35% | The extant Thomasine composition likely reflects later development around an older core. |
| After 130 CE | 15% | Possible for final redaction, but P.Oxy. 654 argues against treating the unit as purely late. |
