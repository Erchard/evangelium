# Logion 10 Evidence Note

Status: working evidence note v0.1.

## Decision

Fire-casting core: `INCLUDE_WITH_MARKER`.

Full Thomas 10: `UNCERTAIN`.

## Included Core

English:

> I cast fire upon the world.

Ukrainian:

> Я кинув вогонь на світ.

## Coptic Witness

Source: `02_Sources_And_Manuscripts/primary_texts/coptic/linssen-thomas-coptic-unicode-working.txt`.

Thomas 10 preserves a complete short Coptic saying:

> ⲡⲉϫⲉ ⲓ̅ⲥ̅ ϫⲉ ⲁⲉⲓⲛⲟⲩϫⲉ ⲛ̅ⲟⲩⲕⲱϩ̅ⲧ` ⲉϫⲛ̅ ⲡⲕⲟⲥⲙⲟⲥ ⲁⲩⲱ ⲉⲓⲥϩⲏⲏⲧⲉ ϯⲁⲣⲉϩ ⲉⲣⲟϥ` ϣⲁⲛⲧⲉϥϫⲉⲣⲟ

The second clause, "I am watching/guarding it until it blazes," is distinctive and should remain in the apparatus.

## Greek Witness

No loaded Greek Oxyrhynchus witness preserves Thomas 10. Any Greek form must be labeled `Greek retroversion, hypothetical`.

## Synoptic Control

Primary control: Luke 12:49.

SBLGNT text:

> Πῦρ ἦλθον βαλεῖν ... ἐπὶ τὴν γῆν

Luke provides strong support for the fire-casting core. The Lukan wording differs from Thomas:

- Luke has "I came to cast fire upon the earth/land."
- Thomas has "I cast fire upon the world/cosmos."
- Luke continues with a wish/question about the fire already being kindled.
- Thomas continues with Jesus watching/guarding the fire until it blazes.

## Internal Thomas Controls

Thomas 16 combines fire with division, sword, war, and a `monachos` ending. The project has already excluded the fire/sword/war sequence from the Logion 16 reader core because the full unit is composite.

Thomas 82 says that whoever is near Jesus is near the fire, and whoever is far from Jesus is far from the kingdom. This shows that fire also has a Thomasine symbolic role.

These controls support caution: fire is not merely a Lukan parallel but also part of Thomas's own symbolic vocabulary.

## Rationale

Reasons for inclusion of the core:

- The saying is short and memorable.
- Luke 12:49 strongly controls the core motif of casting fire.
- The motif plausibly belongs to an early crisis/apocalyptic sayings tradition.
- The core can be separated from the distinctive Thomas continuation.

Reasons for caution about the full Thomas form:

- No loaded Greek Thomas witness preserves Logion 10.
- The Thomas continuation differs from Luke.
- Fire is also a Thomasine symbolic motif in Logia 16 and 82.
- Direction of dependence between Thomas and Luke remains unresolved.

## Probability Assessment
