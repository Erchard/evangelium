## Main Evidence

Thomas 96 preserves the leaven parable: the kingdom is like a woman who hides a little leaven in dough and produces large loaves. Matthew and Luke preserve a close leaven parable in the kingdom-parables tradition.

Thomas's form is compact but has distinctive wording: "kingdom of the Father," "large loaves," and a final hearing formula. The core image is strongly controlled; the frame and ending may be collectional.

## Early-Core Assessment

Possible early core:

> The kingdom is like a woman who hid a little leaven in dough until it worked through the bread.

The woman/leaven/dough transformation image is a plausible early parable core. Thomas's "large loaves" may be a vivid local rendering of the result.

## Secondary-Layer Indicators

"Kingdom of the Father" is a Thomas-favored framing. The final "whoever has ears, let that one hear" may be a collectional or gospel-like performance marker. Dependence on Matthew/Luke remains possible.

## Limits

No extant Greek Thomas witness is loaded. The parable has strong double synoptic control, but the direction of dependence remains unresolved. The clean reader already includes several kingdom parables, so final inclusion should consider balance and duplication.
