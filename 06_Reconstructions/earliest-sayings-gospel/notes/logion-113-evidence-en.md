## Main Evidence

Thomas 113 overlaps strongly with Luke 17:20-21: the kingdom does not come by observation, and people will not say "here" or "there." Thomas then adds that the kingdom of the Father is spread out upon the earth, but people do not see it.

This is important for the project's reconstruction because Logion 3 already includes a related kingdom-not-here-or-there motif. Logion 113 may be a second form of the same tradition, or it may be a later Thomasine clarification of realized/hidden kingdom theology.

## Early-Core Assessment

Possible early core:

> The kingdom does not come by observation; they will not say, "Here" or "There."

The "spread out upon the earth" ending should be treated separately.

## Secondary-Layer Indicators

"Kingdom of the Father" and the hidden-but-present kingdom ending strongly fit Thomas's realized and hidden-revelation themes. The dialogue frame may also be secondary collectional framing.

## Limits

No extant Greek Thomas witness is loaded. Luke is the major external control; internal Thomas control with Logion 3 is strong but also raises the risk of duplication in the clean reader.
