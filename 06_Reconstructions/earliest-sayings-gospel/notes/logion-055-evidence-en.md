# Logion 55 Evidence Note

Status: working evidence note v0.1.

## Decision

`UNCERTAIN`; strong discipleship candidate, but cross language requires caution.

Confidence: low-medium.

## Textual Witnesses

Coptic:

- NHC II,2, local working text.

Greek Thomas witness:

- No loaded Greek Oxyrhynchus Thomas witness preserves Logion 55.
- Any Greek form must be labeled `Greek retroversion, hypothetical`.

Synoptic controls:

- Luke 14:26-27
- Matthew 10:37-38

## Main Evidence

Thomas 55 combines radical family-renunciation language with carrying the cross and being worthy of Jesus. Luke has the closest form for "hate father and mother" plus carrying the cross. Matthew softens the family language into "loving more than me" and also has the worthiness/cross motif.

## Early-Core Assessment

Possible early core:

> Whoever does not relativize family allegiance cannot be my disciple.

The family-renunciation core may be early or shared.

## Secondary-Layer Indicators

The cross clause is the main problem. It may preserve an early metaphor of shame and execution, but it may also reflect post-Easter discipleship language. The full Thomas form should not be treated as unmarked earliest material.

## Limits

No extant Greek Thomas witness is loaded. The cross motif must be evaluated against pre- and post-passion usage.

## Probability Assessment

| Period | Probability | Reason |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Before 60 CE | 20% | Radical discipleship and family conflict may be early. |
| 60-90 CE | 35% | Plausible in early mission and discipleship tradition. |
| 90-130 CE | 35% | Plausible for stabilization of cross-bearing discipleship language. |
| After 130 CE | 10% | Less likely for the whole tradition, possible for final form. |

## Reconstruction Implication

Do not add Logion 55 yet. If later included, consider only a marked discipleship core and keep the cross clause explicitly discussed in the apparatus.
