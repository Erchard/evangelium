## Main Evidence

Thomas 76 joins two related units:

1. a merchant who finds one pearl and sells goods to buy it;
2. an exhortation to seek treasure that does not perish, where moth and worm do not destroy.

Both have strong synoptic controls, but the combined Thomas form is composite.

## Early-Core Assessment

Possible early cores:

> A merchant found one pearl and sold what he had to buy it.

and:

> Seek treasure that does not perish.

The pearl unit is parabolic; the treasure unit is sapiential/exhortational.

## Secondary-Layer Indicators

The chaining of pearl and imperishable treasure may be editorial. The full logion should not be printed as one unmarked primitive unit.

## Limits

No extant Greek Thomas witness is loaded. The relationship to Matthew's pearl parable and treasure teaching remains unresolved.
