# Logia 83, 84, and 85: Image, Preexistence, Adam, And Deathlessness Controls

Status: technical control package v0.1, 2026-07-18.

## Purpose

This package resolves the image/preexistence/Adam `CREATE_CONTROL_FILE` items for Logia 83, 84, and 85. It also clarifies their relationship to Logia 19, 24, 50, and 108.

No clean-reader membership changes are made by this pass.

## Control Boundary

These three logia are internally coherent. They share image, likeness, preexistence, primordial humanity, hidden manifestation, and deathlessness language. That coherence is important for explaining Thomas's theological world, but it is not by itself evidence that the cluster belongs to the earliest recoverable sayings gospel.

The methodological rule is:

- direct manuscript or close synoptic control can support a clean-reader saying;
- internal Thomasine thematic coherence can support appendix interpretation;
- speculative anthropology without external control should not be promoted into the clean reconstructed text.

## Logion 83 Control

Relevant files:

- `corpus/cards/logion-083.md`
- `reconstruction/earliest-sayings-gospel/notes/logion-083-evidence-en.md`

Logion 83 speaks of images visible to people and light hidden in the image of the Father's light. The Coptic text preserves the unit, and the Greek loanword behind `image` is visible through `ϩⲓⲕⲱⲛ`, but that does not create an extant Greek Thomas witness.

Main interpretive options:

1. Vision anthropology: visible forms are not the deepest reality; true light is hidden and must be revealed.
2. Image theology: the human or heavenly image reflects the Father but remains concealed by divine light.
3. Thomasine revelation discourse: the saying belongs to a developed layer where salvation is recognition of hidden identity.
4. Fragment of an earlier image/light saying: possible, but currently not isolatable with enough confidence for the clean reader.

Control decision:

- Logion 83 has no loaded extant Greek Thomas witness.
- It has no direct canonical wording control.
- Logia 24 and 50 provide internal light/origin parallels, and Logion 84 continues the image cluster.
- The evidence supports appendix explanation, not clean-reader inclusion.

Reconstruction implication: keep Logion 83 outside the clean reader. If a Greek form is offered later, mark it as `Greek retroversion, hypothetical`.

## Logion 84 Control

Relevant files:

- `corpus/cards/logion-084.md`
- `reconstruction/earliest-sayings-gospel/notes/logion-084-evidence-en.md`

Logion 84 contrasts seeing one's present likeness with seeing preexistent images that neither die nor become manifest. This is one of the clearest image/preexistence formulations in the corpus.

Main interpretive options:

1. Preexistence anthropology: the true self or heavenly image exists before ordinary embodied identity.
2. Revelation crisis: seeing one's visible likeness is easy; seeing the hidden, deathless image is overwhelming.
3. Thomasine identity theology: salvation is not moral reform alone but recognition of a concealed origin.
4. Early wisdom fragment expanded by later anthropology: possible, but the current wording does not isolate such a fragment.

Control decision:

- Logion 84 has no loaded extant Greek Thomas witness.
- It has no direct canonical control for the full image/preexistence formulation.
- Logia 83 and 85 form the closest internal controls.
- Logion 19 gives a related deathlessness/preexistence horizon, but it also belongs to the high-risk Thomasine anthropology layer.

Reconstruction implication: keep Logion 84 outside the clean reader. Use it in the appendix to explain the developed image/preexistence layer.

## Logion 85 Control

Relevant files:

- `corpus/cards/logion-085.md`
- `reconstruction/earliest-sayings-gospel/notes/logion-085-evidence-en.md`

Logion 85 interprets Adam through power, wealth, worthiness, and death. It is scriptural anthropology rather than a controlled public saying of Jesus. The damaged Coptic text also makes confident Greek reconstruction especially risky.

Main interpretive options:

1. Adam critique: primordial humanity had greatness but failed to attain the deathless condition.
2. Anti-prestige reversal: power and wealth do not equal worthiness before life.
3. Thomasine deathlessness discourse: the saying connects Adam to the collection's repeated "not taste death" motif.
4. Pauline or broader early Christian Adam background: useful as context, but not a direct wording control.

Control decision:

- Logion 85 has no loaded extant Greek Thomas witness.
- It has no close synoptic Jesus-saying control.
- Adam traditions, including 1 Corinthians 15, are contextual controls only.
- Logia 1 and 19 show internal deathlessness language, while Logion 84 supplies the image/preexistence neighbor.

Reconstruction implication: keep Logion 85 outside the clean reader. Present it in the appendix as an important Thomasine/early Christian anthropological reflection, not as a recoverable earliest-gospel saying.

## Related Internal Controls

Logion 19 is relevant because it combines preexistence, paradise imagery, and not tasting death. Its relevance strengthens the identification of a Thomasine deathlessness cluster, but it does not automatically promote Logia 83-85 into the clean reader.

Logion 108 is relevant because it speaks of receiving revelation from Jesus and becoming like him, with hidden things being revealed. It belongs near the same theological field, but it is not a direct control for the wording of Logia 83-85.

## Publication Rule

For final reader and appendix:

- Keep Logia 83, 84, and 85 outside the clean reconstructed text.
- Do not present reconstructed Greek for these logia as manuscript evidence.
- In the appendix, explain that exclusion does not mean irrelevance: these logia are important for understanding Thomas's anthropology, but their strongest evidence is internal and theological rather than external and recoverable.
- Use this package as the control reference whenever image, likeness, preexistence, Adam, or deathlessness language is discussed for these three logia.
