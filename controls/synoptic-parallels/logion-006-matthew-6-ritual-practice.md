# Logion 6 Ritual Practice Cluster

Status: working synoptic and early-Christian control table v0.1.

## Purpose

This table tests Logion 6 against related Thomasine ritual sayings and against early Christian controls for fasting, prayer, almsgiving, food, hidden/revealed disclosure, and bridegroom fasting.

The question is not whether every parallel is directly dependent. The immediate aim is to decide whether Logion 6 can be included in the earliest reconstructed sayings gospel, or whether it should remain marked as a complex Thomasine composition.

## Evaluation

The cluster argues against treating the whole of Thomas 6 as a clean early unit. It is best understood as a composite or redactionally arranged saying that joins:

- a ritual-practice question;
- a short ethical prohibition;
- a hidden/revealed maxim also known from Logion 5 and synoptic tradition.

The possible early core remains:

> Do not lie, and do not do what you hate.

However, the cluster does not yet justify including this core in the main reconstructed text. The current decision for Thomas 6 remains `UNCERTAIN`.
