## Logion 8

Thomas 8 and Matthew 13:47-48 share a fishing/net/fish selection field. But the structures differ:

- Matthew compares the kingdom to a net gathering fish of every kind, followed by sorting good from bad.
- Thomas compares the human being to a wise fisher who chooses one large good fish from many small fish.

The overlap is real but not close enough to include Logion 8 in the reader. The Thomas form may preserve an independent parable, but its meaning and early recoverability remain unresolved.

## Logion 20

The mustard-seed core is multiply controlled and short:

> The kingdom is like a mustard seed: it is small, but it grows and gives shelter to the birds of heaven.

The lack of extant Greek Thomas witness keeps the saying marked. Still, the core is now strong enough for marked reader inclusion because it is short, parabolic, widely attested, and not dependent on a later allegorical interpretation.

## Logion 22

The children/kingdom core is strong:

> Little children are like those who enter the kingdom.

The unity-of-opposites expansion is not included. It remains part of the Thomas apparatus because it belongs to the Thomas unity / monachos cluster and has no direct synoptic control.

## Greek Layer

For Logia 20 and 22, Greek reconstructions must be labeled:

`Greek retroversion, hypothetical`

They are not manuscript witnesses.
