## Control Boundary

Logion 114 is not merely "uncertain." It is the project's clearest secondary-exclusion case. The saying is important for the history of Thomas, Mary traditions, Peter traditions, gendered spiritual language, and community boundary debates, but those same features make it unsuitable for the reconstructed earliest sayings gospel.

The methodological rule is:

- exclusion must be explained, not hidden;
- difficult or offensive material remains in the appendix with full source text and rationale;
- late community dispute scenes should not be presented as the clean voice of the earliest recoverable gospel.

## Exclusion Rationale

Relevant files:

- `corpus/cards/logion-114.md`
- `reconstruction/earliest-sayings-gospel/notes/logion-114-evidence-en.md`

Logion 114 presents a dialogue in which Simon Peter says Mary should leave because women are not worthy of life. Jesus responds by saying he will make her male so she may become a living spirit like the male disciples, and concludes that every woman who makes herself male will enter the kingdom of heaven.

Main interpretive options:

1. Late community conflict: Peter and Mary represent competing authority figures, and the saying preserves a dispute over who belongs.
2. Symbolic transformation: "becoming male" may function as symbolic language for spiritual completion in a community whose symbolic norm is male.
3. Polemical correction: Jesus's response may be intended to overturn Peter's exclusion, but it does so through a problematic transformation formula.
4. Redactional ending: the final position and developed dialogue form may mark the saying as a concluding editorial unit.

Control decision:

- Logion 114 has no loaded extant Greek Thomas witness.
- It has no direct synoptic wording control for the gender-transformation conclusion.
- Logion 22 provides internal male/female transformation language, but Logion 22's unity motif is not the same as Logion 114's exclusion scene.
- Logion 13 gives broad disciple-authority context, not direct wording control.
- The unit is best classified as secondary community/theological material.

Reconstruction implication: exclude Logion 114 from the clean reader. Keep it in the appendix as a transparent example of excluded secondary material.

## Reader-Facing Explanation Rule

The appendix should avoid two bad readings:

1. Treating the exclusion as censorship. The text remains available with Coptic source, translation, and rationale.
2. Treating the saying as straightforwardly representative of the earliest reconstructed gospel. The project's evidence does not support that.

The reader-facing explanation should say plainly that the logion is historically important, but its combination of Peter/Mary conflict, gendered worthiness, and transformation into male life belongs to a later interpretive and community-boundary layer.

## Greek And Source Policy

The Coptic NHC II text is the preserved local witness. No extant Greek Thomas witness is loaded for Logion 114. Because the unit is excluded as secondary, Greek retroversion is not a priority; if one is ever supplied, it must be marked as `Greek retroversion, hypothetical`.

## Publication Rule

- Include the logion in the appendix with full source apparatus and respectful critical explanation.
- Use it in the evidence dossier as an example of how exclusion decisions are made.
- Keep the rationale focused on evidence and layer analysis, not polemical dismissal.
