## Control Boundary

Logion 88 is not controlled by a close synoptic parallel or by an extant Greek Thomas witness in the loaded corpus. Its nearest controls are thematic: prophets, coming figures, reception, giving, and rightful possession.

## Logion 88 Control

Relevant files:

- `corpus/cards/logion-088.md`
- `reconstruction/earliest-sayings-gospel/notes/logion-088-evidence-en.md`

Logion 88 says that angels and prophets come to the hearers and give them what is theirs; the hearers must also give what is in their hands and ask when those figures will come and take what belongs to them. The surface grammar is clear enough, but the referent is not.

Main interpretive options:

1. Revelation-transmission reading: heavenly or prophetic messengers return to the community what already belongs to it.
2. Stewardship reading: the hearers temporarily hold something that belongs to the messengers or to God.
3. Eschatological visitation reading: the coming of angels and prophets tests whether the hearers will return what is not truly theirs.
4. Community authority reading: the saying reflects later disputes about revelation, possession, and legitimate transmission.

Control decision:

- Logion 88 has no loaded extant Greek Thomas witness.
- It has no direct canonical wording control.
- Logion 52 supplies a prophet/living-one contrast, but not direct wording control.
- Logion 21 supplies only a broad motif of vigilance before someone comes.
- The reciprocal "what is yours / what is theirs" language remains too opaque for clean-reader inclusion.

Reconstruction implication: keep Logion 88 outside the clean reader. In the appendix, present the interpretive range instead of forcing one meaning into the reconstructed text.

## Greek And Source Policy
