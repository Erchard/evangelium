# Logia 65-66: Tenants and Rejected Stone Controls

Status: split-core control table v0.1.

## Short Conclusion

Logia 65-66 form an important but risky cluster. The tenants parable and rejected-stone saying have strong Mark/Matthew/Luke and Psalm 118 controls, but the son/heir and rejected-stone interpretation are highly allegorizable and may reflect post-Easter interpretation.

Do not promote this cluster to the clean reader in the next pass.

## Logion 65: Tenants

Thomas core:

> A vineyard owner leased the vineyard to tenants, sent servants for fruit, and finally sent his son; the tenants killed the son.

Controls:

- Mark 12:1-12
- Matthew 21:33-46
- Luke 20:9-19

Assessment:

- Strong narrative control.
- Thomas is shorter than the synoptic forms in some respects.
- The son/heir structure is central and creates a strong allegorical/passion-risk.

Decision: `KEEP_APPENDIX_ONLY_FOR_NOW`; high-priority appendix explanation, not reader promotion.

## Logion 66: Rejected Stone

Thomas core:

> Show me the stone rejected by the builders; it is the cornerstone.

Controls:

- Psalm 118:22
- Mark 12:10-11
- Matthew 21:42
- Luke 20:17

Assessment:

- Very strong scriptural and synoptic control.
- As an isolated saying, it is more scriptural interpretation than simple early aphorism.
- Its placement beside Logion 65 likely functions as interpretive closure.

Decision: `KEEP_APPENDIX_ONLY_FOR_NOW`; do not promote until the project decides whether scriptural proof-text sayings belong in the clean reconstruction.
