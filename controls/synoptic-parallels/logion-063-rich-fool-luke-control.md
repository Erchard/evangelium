# Logion 63: Rich Fool / Storage and Death Control

Status: split-core control table v0.1.

## Short Conclusion

Logion 63 is one of the strongest remaining non-included candidates. It is close to Luke 12:16-21, but the Thomas version is shorter and less explicitly moralized. The short core should be marked as a candidate for a later clean-reader pass.

## Thomas Core

Possible reader core:

> A rich man planned to use his goods, sow, reap, plant, and fill his storehouses so that he would lack nothing. That night he died.

## Luke Control

Luke 12:16-21 has the same narrative architecture:

- rich man's abundance;
- planning storage;
- future security;
- sudden death;
- exposure of wealth-security as folly.

Luke adds divine address and an explicit moral conclusion about storing treasure for oneself rather than being rich toward God. Thomas has a shorter narrative and a hearing formula.

## Direction of Dependence

Possibilities:

1. Thomas preserves a shorter parable core and Luke expands it.
2. Thomas extracts and abbreviates Luke's rich-fool parable.
3. Both depend on a mobile anti-wealth parable tradition.

The current evidence cannot decide direction securely. The shorter Thomas form is promising but not enough for unmarked inclusion.

## Decision

- Short rich-fool core: `PROMOTE_CANDIDATE_FOR_READER_PASS`, medium confidence.
- Full Thomas form with hearing formula: `UNCERTAIN`; keep formula outside any clean-reader core unless the editorial policy keeps hearing formulae.

## Greek Status

No loaded extant Greek Thomas witness. Any Greek form must be marked `Greek retroversion, hypothetical`.
