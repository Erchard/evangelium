# Logion 16: Division Core Final Controls

Status: evidence table v0.1.

## Purpose

This table finalizes the decision on whether a short Logion 16 core can enter the reconstruction while excluding the Thomasine `monachos` ending.

## Layer Separation

| Layer | Thomas 16 | Main controls | Decision |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| A. Not peace but division | People think Jesus came to cast peace; he came to cast divisions | Matthew 10:34; Luke 12:51 | `INCLUDE_WITH_MARKER` |
| B. Household divided | Five in one house; three against two and two against three; father against son | Luke 12:52-53; Matthew 10:35-36 | `INCLUDE_WITH_MARKER` as part of short core |
| C. Fire / sword / war | Fire, sword, war | Luke 12:49 for fire; Matthew 10:34 for sword; no direct control for war | Apparatus only |
| D. Monachos ending | They will stand as `monachos` | Thomas unity / monachos cluster | Exclude from reader |

## Greek Layer

No loaded extant Greek Thomas witness preserves Logion 16.

Any Greek form in the project must be labeled:

`Greek retroversion, hypothetical`

Suggested control shape, not a manuscript witness:

> οὐκ ἦλθον βαλεῖν εἰρήνην ἀλλὰ διαμερισμόν· ἔσονται πέντε ἐν ἑνὶ οἴκῳ διαμεμερισμένοι.

This is controlled mainly by Luke 12:51-52 and secondarily by Matthew 10:34-36.

## Reconstruction Implication

Include the short marked core:

> I did not come to bring peace, but division; a household will be divided.

Do not include the full Thomas form, the fire/sword/war sequence, or the `monachos` ending in the reader text.
