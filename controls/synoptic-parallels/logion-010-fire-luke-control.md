# Logion 10: Fire-Casting Saying and Luke 12:49

Статус: синоптична evidence table v0.1.

## Short Decision

Fire-casting core: `INCLUDE_WITH_MARKER`.

Full Thomas 10: `UNCERTAIN`.

Greek status: no loaded P.Oxy. witness preserves Thomas 10. Any Greek form must be labeled `Greek retroversion, hypothetical`.

## Reconstructed Core

English:

> I cast fire upon the world.

Ukrainian:

> Я кинув вогонь на світ.

This is a marked reconstruction of the core, not the full Coptic Thomas 10.

## Textual Controls

| Source | Text / motif | Assessment |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Thomas 10, Coptic | Jesus casts fire on the world and watches/guards it until it blazes | Complete Coptic witness; no loaded Greek Thomas witness. |
| Luke 12:49 | `Πῦρ ἦλθον βαλεῖν ... ἐπὶ τὴν γῆν` | Strong synoptic control for the fire-casting core. |
| Luke 12:50-53 | Baptism, division, divided household | Shows the wider Lukan context where fire leads into crisis/division. |
| Thomas 16 | Not peace but division; fire, sword, war | Internal Thomas control; shows the fire motif can be combined with division material. |
| Thomas 82 | Near Jesus / near fire; far from Jesus / far from kingdom | Internal Thomas control; shows fire is also a Thomasine symbolic motif. |

| Fire already kindled / blazing | Thomas: watches until it blazes | Luke: wishes/questions if already kindled | Related but not identical; apparatus only. |

## Rationale

The minimal fire-casting core is short, memorable, and strongly controlled by Luke 12:49. The saying plausibly belongs to a mobile apocalyptic or crisis-saying tradition.

The full Thomas form should remain outside the reader text for now. Its "watching/guarding until it blazes" continuation differs from Luke and may be an independent variant, a Thomasine symbolic reshaping, or a secondary development. Thomas 16 and Thomas 82 show that fire is meaningful inside Thomas, which strengthens the need for caution.

## Publication Implication

Print only the short fire-casting core in the Ukrainian reader with a visible marker. Keep the continuation in the apparatus.
