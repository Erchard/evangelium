## Logion 40: Plant Not Planted By The Father

Relevant local files:

- `corpus/cards/logion-040.md`
- `reconstruction/earliest-sayings-gospel/notes/logion-040-evidence-en.md`

Main canonical control:

> Matthew 15:13: `ὁ δὲ ἀποκριθεὶς εἶπεν· Πᾶσα φυτεία ἣν οὐκ ἐφύτευσεν ὁ πατήρ μου ὁ οὐράνιος ἐκριζωθήσεται.`

Thomas 40 is close enough to Matthew 15:13 to matter. The core image is the same kind of warning: what the Father did not plant will be uprooted. The local Coptic form, however, speaks of a vine/plant planted outside the Father and not firmly rooted.

Control decision:

- Treat Matthew 15:13 as a strong canonical control, not as automatic proof of Thomas independence.
- Keep three possibilities visible: Thomas may preserve a compact independent form; Thomas may depend on Matthew; both may draw from a shared plant-not-Father warning.
- Because there is no loaded Greek Thomas witness for Logion 40, any Greek Thomas wording must be labeled `Greek retroversion, hypothetical`.

Reconstruction implication: Logion 40 remains outside the clean reader. Its appendix section should present the Coptic form, Matthew 15:13 in Greek, and the unresolved direction-of-dependence problem.

## Logia 48 And 106: Agreement, Unity, And Mountain-Moving

Relevant local files:

- `corpus/cards/logion-048.md`
- `corpus/cards/logion-106.md`
- `reconstruction/earliest-sayings-gospel/notes/logion-048-evidence-en.md`
- `reconstruction/earliest-sayings-gospel/notes/logion-106-evidence-en.md`
- `reconstruction/earliest-sayings-gospel/notes/thomas-unity-monachos-cluster-en.md`

Main canonical controls:

- Matthew 18:19-20 controls the "two agreeing / gathered" field.
- Mark 11:23, Matthew 17:20, and Matthew 21:21 control the "say to this mountain, move" field.

Local canonical Greek already transcribed in the cards:

> Matthew 18:20: `οὗ γάρ εἰσιν δύο ἢ τρεῖς συνηγμένοι εἰς τὸ ἐμὸν ὄνομα, ἐκεῖ εἰμι ἐν μέσῳ αὐτῶν.`

> Mark 11:23: `... ὃς ἂν εἴπῃ τῷ ὄρει τούτῳ· Ἄρθητι καὶ βλήθητι εἰς τὴν θάλασσαν ...`

Thomas 48 says that if two make peace with one another in one house, they can tell the mountain to move. Thomas 106 says that when the two are made one, the hearers become "sons of man" and can tell the mountain to move.

Control decision:

- The mountain-moving motif has strong external control.
- The agreement/two motif has external control in Matthew 18:19-20.
- The "make the two one" condition belongs to the Thomas unity/monachos cluster and is a caution marker for Thomasine reshaping.
- Logion 48 and Logion 106 should be read together: each helps show how Thomas can fuse external Jesus tradition with its own unity language.

Reconstruction implication: neither Logion 48 nor Logion 106 enters the clean reader in this pass. A future split-core pass may test whether a short mountain-moving core or two-in-agreement core has independent value, but the current Thomas compositions remain appendix-only/deferred.

## Logion 62: Right Hand, Left Hand, And Mystery Frame

Relevant local files:

- `corpus/cards/logion-062.md`
- `reconstruction/earliest-sayings-gospel/notes/logion-062-evidence-en.md`
- `controls/synoptic-parallels/logion-006-matthew-6-ritual-practice.md`
- `reconstruction/earliest-sayings-gospel/notes/ritual-ethics-cluster-006-014-027-104-en.md`

Main canonical control:

> Matthew 6:3: `σοῦ δὲ ποιοῦντος ἐλεημοσύνην μὴ γνώτω ἡ ἀριστερά σου τί ποιεῖ ἡ δεξιά σου,`

The second half of Thomas 62 is strongly controlled by Matthew 6:3. The first half, however, says that Jesus reveals mysteries to those worthy of his mysteries. That frame fits Thomas's wider secrecy and revelation profile, especially near Logia 1 and 13.

Control decision:

- Treat the right/left hand saying as a controlled ethical secrecy subunit.
- Do not treat the full Thomas 62 form as a controlled early unit.
- The mystery/worthy-recipient frame should remain apparatus material and be discussed as Thomasine framing.
- Since no loaded Greek Thomas witness exists for Logion 62, Greek Thomas wording remains hypothetical.

Reconstruction implication: Logion 62 remains outside the clean reader. The appendix should separate the ethical secrecy saying from the mystery-disclosure frame and explain why the controlled subunit is still not promoted here.
