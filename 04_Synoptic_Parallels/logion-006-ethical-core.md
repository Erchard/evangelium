# Logion 6 Ethical Core: Do Not Lie / Do Not Do What You Hate

Status: evidence table v0.1.

## Unit Tested

This table tests only the possible ethical core of Thomas 6:

> Do not lie, and do not do what you hate.

It does not test the whole Thomas 6 composition. The ritual-practice question and the hidden/revealed ending remain outside the reader reconstruction.

## Thomas Witnesses

| Witness | Textual status | Note |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Coptic NHC II Thomas 6 | Complete | Preserves the ethical answer after a question about fasting, prayer, almsgiving, and food. |
| Greek P.Oxy. 654 | Important but lacunose | DCLP reconstructs `μὴ ψεύδεσθε καὶ ὃτι μισεῖτε μὴ ποιεῖτε`; the phrase is partly supplied in lost text. |

## Jewish and Scriptural Controls

| Source | Control | Relation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Tobit 4:15 | `καὶ ὃ μισεῖς, μηδενὶ ποιήσῃς` | Strongest control for the second half of the Thomas core. It shows the negative Golden Rule in Jewish sapiential form. |
| Leviticus 19:11 | `οὐ ψεύσεσθε` | Strong Torah control for the prohibition of lying. |
| Zechariah 8:16-17 | speak truth to one another; do not love false oath; "these things I hate" | Good thematic control joining truthfulness, falsehood, and hated conduct. |

## Synoptic and Early-Christian Controls

| Source | Control | Relation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Matthew 7:12 | positive Golden Rule | Positive reciprocity form; supports the broad ethical field but is not the same wording as Thomas 6. |
| Luke 6:31 | positive Golden Rule | Positive reciprocity form; supports broad circulation in Jesus tradition. |
| Didache 1:2 | negative reciprocity rule | Early Christian control for a form closer to Tobit and Thomas than Matthew/Luke. |
| Colossians 3:9 | `μὴ ψεύδεσθε εἰς ἀλλήλους` | Early Christian control for direct "do not lie" wording, though not a Jesus saying. |

## Assessment

The short ethical core has a much stronger profile than the whole Thomas 6 composition.

The phrase "do not do what you hate" has a direct Jewish sapiential control in Tobit 4:15 and belongs to the negative Golden Rule tradition. The prohibition of lying has strong Torah and early-Christian controls. Matthew and Luke preserve the positive reciprocity form, while Didache preserves an early Christian form closer to the negative rule.

The main caution is textual and compositional: P.Oxy. 654 is lacunose at the key phrase, and the extant Thomas unit embeds the ethical core inside a ritual-practice question and hidden/revealed ending.

## Reconstruction Implication

Decision for the short ethical core: `INCLUDE_WITH_MARKER`.

Decision for the full Logion 6 composition: remains `UNCERTAIN` and should not be added to the reader text.
