# Logion 54: Blessed Are the Poor

Status: synoptic control table v0.1.

## Short Conclusion

Thomas 54 is one of the strongest short candidates for `INCLUDE_WITH_MARKER`. Its closest control is Luke 6:20; Matthew 5:3 preserves a related but interpretively expanded "poor in spirit" form.

## Reconstruction Decision

Decision: `INCLUDE_WITH_MARKER`.

Reason: the short blessing of the poor is compact, multiply controlled, and likely belongs to early beatitude tradition. It remains marked because the loaded Thomas evidence is Coptic only and because Thomas combines Luke-like direct poverty with Matthew-like "kingdom of heaven."

## Direction of Dependence

Thomas 54 combines features that align with Luke and Matthew but does not simply copy either. The shortness of the saying and lack of Matthean "in spirit" make a pre-Matthean or independent beatitude core plausible. The "kingdom of heaven" wording may be inherited from a variant tradition, influenced by Matthew, or introduced in Thomas/Coptic transmission.

Direction remains unresolved.
