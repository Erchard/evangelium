# Logion 72: Inheritance-Dispute Refusal

Status: synoptic control table v0.1.

## Short Conclusion

Thomas 72 has a very close control in Luke 12:13-14. The Thomas version is shorter and omits Luke 12:15, the explicit warning against greed. This makes the logion a plausible `INCLUDE_WITH_MARKER`, but dependence on Luke remains a major risk.

## Reconstruction Decision

Decision: `INCLUDE_WITH_MARKER`.

Reason: the core refusal is compact and memorable; Thomas may preserve a shorter dispute scene. Marked status is required because there is no loaded Greek Thomas witness and because Luke is the only strong external control.

## Direction of Dependence

Thomas 72 could be a shortened form of Luke 12:13-15, especially because the scene is otherwise uncommon. It could also preserve a compact refusal story that Luke expands with anti-greed application and the rich-fool parable.

The decisive risk is single-control dependence: unlike Logia 41, 54, and 73, this unit has one strong synoptic control, not broad multiple attestation.

## Possible Early Core

> A man asked Jesus to divide an inheritance. Jesus said, "Who made me a divider?"

## Publication Implication

Thomas 72 can move to `INCLUDE_WITH_MARKER`, but it should be introduced into the reader only after the apparatus clearly warns that Luke-dependence remains possible. The clean reader may print the short refusal scene without Luke's anti-greed application.
