# Split-Core Decision Review B v0.1

Status: completed review pass.

Date: 2026-07-17.

## Purpose

This review closes the second major split-core gap identified by the final all-114 decision audit. The target logia are strong enough to deserve more than a simple `UNCERTAIN` label, but not strong enough to print automatically in the clean reconstruction as full Thomas logia.

The review asks a narrower question:

> Is there a smaller core that should be considered in a later controlled clean-reader candidate pass?

The clean Ukrainian reader is not edited in this pass.

## Method

Each logion was checked against:

- its corpus card;
- its evidence note;
- its synoptic/control file;
- internal Thomas duplication risks;
- the project's Greek witness policy.

Where no loaded extant Greek Thomas witness exists, any Greek wording remains only a `Greek retroversion, hypothetical`.

## Reader-Candidate Implications

The next controlled clean-reader candidate pass should consider, but not automatically include:

- 76A: a merchant finds one pearl and sells what he has to buy it;
- 76B: seek treasure that does not perish;
- 78A: wilderness, reed, and soft-clothing core;
- 79A: blessed are those who hear and keep the word;
- 103A: watchfulness before the thief comes.

The pass should not add the full forms of Logia 76, 78, 79, or 103. It should also not add Logia 94, 109, or 113 without a separate duplication/dependence argument.

## Appendix Implications

The appendix should preserve all target logia, including excluded or non-promoted subunits, because readers need to see why they are not part of the clean reconstruction.

Especially important appendix explanations:

- Logion 94 is strong as tradition but redundant inside this reconstruction because Logion 2 already carries the seek/find motif.
- Logion 109 is attractive as a parable but too expanded and too dependent-risky for clean-reader promotion.
- Logion 113 has strong controls but overlaps heavily with Logion 3 and carries a distinctly Thomasine hidden-presence ending.
