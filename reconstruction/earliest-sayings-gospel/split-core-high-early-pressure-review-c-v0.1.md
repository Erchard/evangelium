## Purpose

This review handles the strongest high-early-score logia that are still outside the clean reader after the probability-pressure audit:

- 46: John the Baptist and the least/small one in the kingdom.
- 90: yoke and rest invitation.
- 91: recognizing sky/earth but not the time.
- 103: thief and watchfulness.
- 104: fasting and the bridegroom.

The clean reader is not edited in this pass. The task is to decide whether these units should become controlled reader candidates, remain appendix-only, or wait for cluster-level handling.

## Logion 46

Logion 46 is the strongest positive candidate in this package. The core contrast between John and the least in the kingdom is controlled by both Matthew 11:11 and Luke 7:28. This is not merely a vague thematic parallel.

The main caution is the Thomas form. "The one who becomes small among you will know the kingdom" may have been shaped by Thomas's knowledge vocabulary and by the already included small-children / kingdom core in Logion 22. That overlap does not make Logion 46 redundant, because Logion 46 preserves a distinct John-the-Baptist boundary saying. It does, however, require a split-core reader decision rather than automatic inclusion.

Recommendation: promote only the short John/least-in-kingdom core to the next controlled clean-reader candidate pass.

## Logion 90

Logion 90 has a high early score because its central language is traditional: coming to Jesus, yoke, gentleness, and rest. The problem is that the control is almost entirely Matthew 11:28-30. There is no loaded Greek Thomas witness and no Mark/Luke double control for the specific form.

This makes Logion 90 weaker than several already included units with broader or cleaner control. It may be important for reception history and for explaining Thomas's relation to Matthean-like wisdom Christology, but it should not be promoted now.

Recommendation: keep appendix-only.

## Logion 91

Logion 91 must be split. The opening question, "Tell us who you are so that we may believe," is a christological dialogue frame. The second half, which criticizes people for reading sky and earth but not recognizing the time, has strong controls in Luke 12:54-56 and Matthew 16:2-3.

The recognition-of-time core deserves reader-candidate pressure, but only after it is handled as a split unit. The full logion should not be printed.

Recommendation: promote the recognition-of-time core to a later controlled reader-candidate pass; keep the full logion appendix-only.

## Logion 103

Logion 103 has a strong thief/watchfulness core and good Matt/Luke controls. But this exact issue has already appeared in Split-Core Review B and the controlled clean-reader candidate pass. The previous decision was cautious: keep it out until the Logion 21 / 103 thief-watchfulness cluster is handled.

That decision still stands. Logion 103 should not be promoted alone, because Logion 21 contains a larger composite thief/watchfulness/harvest unit and may be the more important control problem.

Recommendation: defer to a Logion 21 / 103 cluster-control pass before any reader decision.

## Logion 104

Logion 104 has strong bridegroom-fasting controls in Mark 2:18-20, Matthew 9:14-15, and Luke 5:33-35. The possible early core is real.

The extant Thomas form, however, is not just the synoptic bridegroom saying. It begins with a proposal to pray and fast, answers with a difficult question about sin or being overcome, and then speaks of the bridegroom leaving the bridal chamber. That framing belongs to the ritual-ethics and bridal-chamber field of Thomas and may be secondary.

Recommendation: keep the full logion appendix-only; defer any short bridegroom-fasting core until a ritual-ethics / bridegroom-focused control pass.

## Clean-Reader Implication

No clean-reader change is made in this review.

The next controlled reader-candidate pass should consider:

- Logion 91A: recognition-of-time core.

It should not consider Logia 90, 103, or 104 as direct additions until their dependence, duplication, and cluster issues are resolved.
