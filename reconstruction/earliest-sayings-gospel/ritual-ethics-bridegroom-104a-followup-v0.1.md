## Question

Should a short Logion 104A core be promoted to the clean reconstruction?

Possible core:

> When the bridegroom leaves, then they may fast and pray.

## Evidence Considered

- Coptic Thomas: full Logion 104 is preserved in NHC II,2.
- Greek Thomas papyri: no loaded P.Oxy. 1, P.Oxy. 654, or P.Oxy. 655 witness for this logion.
- Greek layer: only `Greek retroversion, hypothetical`.
- Canonical controls: Mark 2:18-20, Matthew 9:14-15, Luke 5:33-35.
- Internal Thomas controls: Logia 6, 14, and 27 in the ritual-ethics cluster.
- Prior review: `split-core-high-early-pressure-review-c-v0.1.md`.

## Rationale

The bridegroom-fasting motif is strong, old-looking, and important. It belongs to a recognizable early Christian debate about when fasting is appropriate in relation to the presence or absence of the bridegroom.

That does not make the Thomas 104 form a secure clean-reader unit.

The preserved Thomas form adds several layers:

- a proposal to pray and fast today;
- Jesus' difficult question about sin or being overcome;
- the bridegroom leaving the bridal chamber;
- a final command to fast and pray.

These elements pull the saying into the Thomas ritual-ethics and bridal-chamber field. The strongest recoverable wording for the bridegroom-fasting motif comes from Mark/Matthew/Luke rather than from an extant Greek Thomas witness. Promoting 104A would therefore risk importing the canonical form into the reconstruction and presenting it as if Thomas independently preserved a clean early core.

## Why Not Promote 104A

The clean reader is selective, not maximal. It should not include every early-looking or canonically controlled motif.

104A fails the current clean-reader threshold because:

- no extant Greek Thomas witness is loaded;
- the Coptic Thomas form is embedded in a broader ritual/bridal-chamber frame;
- the short recoverable core is too dependent on canonical controls;
- the motif is already well known from the canonical tradition, so adding it would not substantially improve the reconstruction;
- appendix treatment can preserve the evidence honestly without overstating independence.

## Publication Implication

In the appendix, Logion 104 should be treated seriously:

- explain the strong canonical bridegroom-fasting controls;
- print or cite the Coptic witness and mark the Greek layer as hypothetical;
- explain the sin/overcome and bridal-chamber framing;
- state that the saying is excluded from the clean reconstruction because the recoverable Thomas form is not sufficiently independent or clean.

In the clean reader, no 104A unit should be added unless new source evidence appears.
