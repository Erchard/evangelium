# Greek Retroversion Confidence Audit v0.1

Status: working audit, 2026-07-17.

## Purpose

This audit prevents the project's Greek layer from being misunderstood. Some Greek text is materially witnessed in P.Oxy. 1, 654, or 655. Some is extant but lacunose or heavily restored. Some is only a cautious Greek retroversion from Coptic, controlled by synoptic or other evidence.

Retroversions are not manuscript witnesses.

## Categories

- `Extant Greek witness` - materially preserved in a loaded Greek Thomas papyrus witness.
- `Lacunose extant Greek witness` - preserved in a Greek papyrus witness but dependent on substantial restoration.
- `Partial extant Greek witness` - only part of the reader unit is preserved in Greek.
- `Extant Greek plus hypothetical retroversion` - one subunit is extant, another is retroverted.
- `Greek retroversion, hypothetical` - no loaded Greek Thomas witness; proposed Greek form is a controlled back-translation.

## Publication Rule

In any public-facing edition:

1. P.Oxy. text must retain brackets and lacuna warnings.
2. Partial witnesses must specify exactly what is extant.
3. Every non-extant Greek reconstruction must be introduced as `Greek retroversion, hypothetical`.
4. Retroversions may support comparison, but must not be counted as manuscript evidence.
