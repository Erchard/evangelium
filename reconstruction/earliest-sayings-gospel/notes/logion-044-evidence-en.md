# Logion 44 Evidence Note

Status: working evidence note v0.1.

## Decision

`UNCERTAIN`; possible early blasphemy-Spirit core, but not ready for reader inclusion.

Confidence: low-medium.

## Textual Witnesses

Coptic:

- NHC II,2, local working text.

Greek Thomas witness:

- No loaded Greek Oxyrhynchus Thomas witness preserves Logion 44.
- Any Greek form must be labeled `Greek retroversion, hypothetical`.

Synoptic controls:

- Mark 3:28-29
- Matthew 12:31-32
- Luke 12:10

## Main Evidence

Thomas 44 belongs to the blasphemy-against-the-Spirit tradition. The core contrast is broadly shared: some speech against a figure may be forgiven, but speech against the Holy Spirit is not forgiven.

Matthew and Luke preserve a Son-of-Man contrast. Thomas has Father / Son / Holy Spirit language, which gives the saying a more explicitly triadic shape.

## Early-Core Assessment

Possible early core:

> Blasphemy against the Holy Spirit will not be forgiven.

This core has strong synoptic support and may reflect an early controversy saying.

## Secondary-Layer Indicators

The full Thomas form is not yet secure as early because:

- Father / Son / Holy Spirit sequencing may reflect later theological shaping;
- no extant Greek Thomas witness is loaded for this logion;
- direction of dependence between Thomas and the synoptic tradition is unresolved.

## Limits

This note does not establish Thomasine independence. A line-by-line study of the Mark/Matthew/Luke forms is still needed before any reader-text inclusion.

## Reconstruction Implication

Do not add Logion 44 to the clean reader yet. Keep it as a high-value uncertain control. If included later, only a short Spirit-blasphemy core should be considered, not the full triadic form without marker.
