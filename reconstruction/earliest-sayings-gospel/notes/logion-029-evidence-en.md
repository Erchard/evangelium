# Logion 29 Evidence Note

Status: evidence rationale v0.1.

## Decision

`DEFER`; keep outside the clean reader.

## Working Unit

> Flesh, spirit, body, wealth, and poverty are presented as a paradox.

## Textual Witnesses

- Coptic base: extant NHC II,2; primary witness for the full saying.
- P.Oxy. 1: tiny overlap, apparently preserving only the final poverty phrase.
- P.Oxy. 654: not preserved.
- P.Oxy. 655: not preserved.
- Canonical controls: no close synoptic control; Pauline body/spirit and treasure/poverty language is thematic only.

## Main Evidence

The Coptic saying is coherent but abstract. P.Oxy. 1 provides direct but minimal Greek control for the final "poverty" clause only. It does not verify the full flesh/spirit/body logic.

## Reason For Non-Inclusion

The full unit looks like developed anthropological reflection. Without stronger external control or a clear short core, it should not be treated as part of the earliest recoverable sayings gospel.

## Reconstruction Implication

Keep Logion 29 in the appendix. Any Greek version beyond the tiny P.Oxy. 1 overlap must be labeled hypothetical.

## Remaining Work

Prepare a controlled Greek retroversion only for research use, and test whether the final wealth-in-poverty aphorism can be evaluated separately from the full flesh/spirit speculation.
