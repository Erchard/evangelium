# Logion 14 Evidence Rationale v0.1

## Decision

Current publication decision: `DEFER_TO_CLUSTER`.

Reader implication: do not include Logion 14 in the clean reconstruction as a full saying. Its components remain important for the appendix and dossier, especially the defilement saying and mission instruction.

## Source Basis

- Coptic base: extant in NHC II,2 and used as the primary preserved witness.
- P.Oxy. status: no loaded Oxyrhynchus Greek witness for this logion.
- Greek policy: any Greek form must be treated as a hypothetical retroversion from the Coptic and from canonical controls.
- Canonical controls: Matthew 6:1-18 for alms/prayer/fasting; Mark 7:15 and Matthew 15:11 for defilement; Luke 10:8-9 for eating what is set before one and healing the sick.
- Internal controls: Logia 6 and 104 for ritual practice; the ritual-ethics cluster file already treats this as cluster material.

## Rationale

Logion 14 is not a single clean unit for earliest-gospel reconstruction. It combines at least three distinguishable blocks: critique of fasting/prayer/almsgiving, mission instruction about food and healing, and a defilement saying about what enters and exits the mouth.

The final defilement block has strong canonical control and may preserve or echo an early tradition. The mission block also overlaps with early mission tradition. But the full Thomas form has a compiled shape, and the opening attack on ritual practices is best handled with Logia 6 and 104 before any subunit is promoted.

## Appendix Use

Use Logion 14 as a high-value appendix case for showing how a saying can contain early material without being included as a full reconstructed unit. The appendix should separate the ritual, mission, and defilement subunits and explain the different levels of confidence.
