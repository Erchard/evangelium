# Seek / Find Cluster-Control Note v0.1

Status: third cluster-control pass, 2026-07-18.

## Scope

This note controls the seek/find group around Logia 2, 92, and 94.

Primary logia:

- 2: let the one who seeks not stop seeking until they find; finding leads to disturbance, amazement, reigning, and possibly rest.
- 92: seek and you will find, followed by a dialogue-memory expansion about things once asked, not then told, now offered, and no longer sought.
- 94: the one who seeks will find; to the one who knocks, it will be opened.

Related material: Logion 1 uses finding interpretation as a collection frame; Logion 38 contains seek/not-find material. These related items are not primary decision targets in this pass.

## Cluster Diagnosis

The seek/find motif is one of the strongest organizing motifs in the reconstructed reader, but the project should not print every seek/find saying in the clean layer.

The cluster contains three different kinds of material:

1. A programmatic seek/find saying with Greek papyrus support and a developed Thomasine sequence: Logion 2.
2. A seek/find opening embedded in a later collection-memory frame: Logion 92.
3. A compact seek/find and knock/open doublet with strong Matthew/Luke control but likely redundancy: Logion 94.

The best publication decision is selective representation: keep Logion 2 as the main clean-reader witness to the motif, and discuss Logia 92 and 94 in the appendix.

## External Control

Logion 2 has the strongest Thomas evidence because P.Oxy. 654 preserves a Greek form of the saying, though with lacunae and reconstruction. Matthew 7:7 and Luke 11:9 provide a real seek/find control, but Thomas 2 lacks the full ask/seek/knock triad and instead develops a sequence of seeking, finding, disturbance, amazement, reigning, and possibly rest.

Logion 94 has strong canonical control through Matthew 7:7-8 and Luke 11:9-10. Its short two-part form may preserve an early form or a Thomasine abbreviation. The problem is not weakness of motif; the problem is publication economy and possible duplication because Logion 2 already represents seek/find in the clean reader.

Logion 92 has a strong opening core, but the full logion is structurally different. It looks like collectional memory: things once asked but not told, now offered, yet not sought. That makes it important for appendix interpretation but not suitable as a clean reader unit.

## Reader-Layer Implication

No clean-reader expansion is recommended by this pass.

Logion 2 remains the clean reader's main seek/find unit. This avoids overloading the first layer with doublets while preserving Logia 92 and 94 in the appendix for transparent comparison.

## Appendix-Layer Implication

The appendix should treat this cluster as a major explanation of how the project handles repeated traditional motifs:

- A motif can be early and still represented only once in the clean reader.
- P.Oxy. 654 gives Logion 2 a stronger evidential position than Coptic-only Logia 92 and 94.
- Logion 94 is strong as tradition but may be redundant as publication text.
- Logion 92 helps explain Thomas's internal collection memory and should be interpreted, not discarded.

## Evidence Dossier Use

In the English evidence dossier, this cluster should support a methodological section on doublets and publication economy:

- The project distinguishes historical strength from reader-layer necessity.
- A saying may be ancient, meaningful, and well controlled, yet still remain appendix-only if another stronger witness already represents the motif.
- The clean reconstruction is not a maximal anthology of all plausible early material.
