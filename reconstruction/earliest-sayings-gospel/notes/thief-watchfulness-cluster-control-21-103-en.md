# Thief / Watchfulness Cluster-Control: Logia 21 and 103

Status: sixth cluster-control pass, 2026-07-18.

## Scope

This note resolves the deferred thief/watchfulness cluster shared by Thomas 21 and Thomas 103. It does not treat either full Thomas logion as a single early unit. The task is to decide whether a short, controlled reader core can be responsibly printed in the reconstructed earliest sayings gospel.

## Units

## Primary Thomas Evidence

Logion 21 is a composite saying. It moves through children on another's field, returning the field to its owners, a householder who would guard the house before the thief comes, a command to guard against the world, and a harvest image. Because the unit is constructed from several images, it cannot be printed as a single clean early saying.

Logion 103 is narrower. It blesses the one who knows when the thieves are coming and prepares before they enter. The preserved Coptic form also includes gathering one's kingdom/domain and girding oneself. Those additions may be Thomas-specific interpretation of the watchfulness image.

## Canonical Controls

The main controls are Matthew 24:43 and Luke 12:39. Both preserve the householder/thief warning: if the householder knew when the thief was coming, he would have watched and not allowed the house to be broken into. Mark 13:33-37 provides a broader watchfulness control, though not the same thief image. Mark 4:29 controls the harvest subunit in Logion 21, but that is adjacent material rather than the same reader core.

These canonical texts are not Thomas manuscript witnesses. They support a shared early-tradition motif and possible Greek wording, but they do not prove that Thomas preserves an independent pre-synoptic form.

## Reconstruction Decision

Promote only Logion 103A as a marked clean-reader core:

> Blessed is the one who knows when the thief is coming and keeps watch before he comes.

The project should not print Logion 21's thief subunit separately. It is embedded in a larger composite unit and would duplicate 103A without offering a cleaner reader form. Logion 21 should support the cluster argument in the appendix and dossier.

The project should not print the full Logion 103. The gathering-domain / girding expansion remains important for interpreting Thomas, but it is not needed for the earliest recoverable saying.

## Greek Retroversion Policy

No loaded Greek Thomas witness preserves Logion 21 or 103. Any Greek text for 103A must therefore be labeled `Greek retroversion, hypothetical`.

Suggested reader-core retroversion:

> μακάριος ὁ γινώσκων πότε ὁ κλέπτης ἔρχεται καὶ γρηγορεῖ πρὸ τῆς ἐλεύσεως αὐτοῦ.

This retroversion combines the Thomas beatitude shape with synoptic watchfulness vocabulary. It is not an extant papyrus text.

## Reader-Facing Explanation

For a non-specialist reader, the saying is about readiness. The point is not that the disciple can control the crisis, but that the disciple recognizes danger before it arrives. The thief image turns watchfulness into a household scene: if you know the house can be broken into, you do not sleep as if nothing can happen.

The cluster also shows why reconstruction must be selective. Thomas 21 contains a related warning, but it is woven into a larger composition. Thomas 103 has a clearer form, but its full wording has later-looking expansion. The clean reader therefore prints the smallest responsible core and leaves the rest for the appendix.
