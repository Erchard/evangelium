# Ritual-Ethics Cluster: Thomas 6, 14, 27, and 104

Status: working evidence note v0.1.

## Question

Should Thomas 6 be included in the reconstructed earliest sayings gospel, either as a whole or as a short ethical core?

## Cluster Units

| Logion | Core theme | Witness status | Current decision |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 6 | ritual-practice question; ethical prohibition; hidden/revealed ending | Coptic NHC II; Greek P.Oxy. 654, lacunose | ethical core `INCLUDE_WITH_MARKER`; full logion `UNCERTAIN` |
| 14 | critique of fasting, prayer, almsgiving; mission; food defilement | Coptic NHC II; no loaded Greek Oxyrhynchus witness | `DEFER` |
| 27 | fast from the world; make Sabbath a Sabbath | Coptic NHC II; Greek P.Oxy. 1; P.Oxy. 5575 control | `UNCERTAIN` |
| 104 | prayer/fasting and the bridegroom | Coptic NHC II; no loaded Greek Oxyrhynchus witness | `DEFER` |

## Main Finding

The cluster strengthens caution about Thomas 6. Thomas does not preserve a single simple position on fasting, prayer, almsgiving, food, and Sabbath. Instead, the relevant sayings show several moves:

- ritual practice is questioned;
- ritual practice is criticized;
- ritual practice is spiritualized;
- ritual practice is tied to the presence or absence of the bridegroom;
- food purity is redirected toward what comes out of the mouth;
- hidden/revealed language is reused across units.

This makes the full Thomas 6 composition look less like a clean primitive saying and more like a compact Thomasine composition or redactional arrangement.

## Possible Early Core

The most plausible early recoverable element inside Thomas 6 is:

> Do not lie, and do not do what you hate.

This is short, memorable, and ethically direct. A separate ethical-core test now supports including this core with a marker, while the full Logion 6 composition remains `UNCERTAIN`.

## Controls

### Matthew 6:1-18

Matthew provides the closest broad thematic control for almsgiving, prayer, and fasting. Matthew regulates these practices in relation to public display and hidden divine reward. Thomas 6 asks about the same practices but answers with truthfulness, hated action, and hidden/revealed disclosure.

### Food Defilement

Mark 7:15 and Matthew 15:11 are strong controls for Thomas 14, not for Thomas 6 directly. Their relevance to Thomas 6 is mediated by the question about food observance.

### Bridegroom Fasting

Mark 2:18-20, Matthew 9:14-15, and Luke 5:33-35 are strong controls for Thomas 104. This confirms that fasting/prayer discourse circulated in multiple early Christian forms, but it does not make Thomas 6 itself secure.

### Didache 8

Didache 8 regulates fasting days and prayer practice. It is an early Christian control for the ritual-practice field, but its direction is different from Thomas: Didache prescribes communal discipline; Thomas reinterprets ritual practice through ethics, world-renunciation, and symbolic presence.

### Logion 27 Follow-up

Logion 27 has now been given a separate evidence note: `notes/logion-027-evidence-en.md`.

The result moves it from `DEFER` to `UNCERTAIN`. P.Oxy. 1 strongly confirms the saying in Greek, and P.Oxy. 5575 adds a significant early control for related material in a mixed sayings environment. Still, the content remains a symbolic reinterpretation of ritual practice, and the synoptic controls are thematic rather than directly formulaic. Therefore it should not be added to the reconstructed reader text at this stage.

## Decision for Thomas 6

Decision for the full logion remains: `UNCERTAIN`.

Decision for the short ethical core: `INCLUDE_WITH_MARKER`.

Probability assessment remains unchanged:

| Period | Probability | Reason |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Before 60 CE | 10% | A short ethical core could be early, but the extant composition is complex. |
| 60-90 CE | 25% | Possible as a developing sayings cluster around practice and ethics. |
| 90-130 CE | 40% | Strong fit for Thomasine composition and redaction. |
| After 130 CE | 25% | Possible for final shaping, though P.Oxy. 654 preserves a Greek form of the unit. |

## Reconstruction Implication

Do not add the full Thomas 6 composition to the main reconstructed text.

Add only the short ethical core with a marker:

> Do not lie, and do not do what you hate.
