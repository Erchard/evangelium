## Decision

Current publication decision: `APPENDIX_ONLY_UNCERTAIN`.

## Source Basis

- Coptic base: extant in NHC II,2.
- P.Oxy. status: no loaded Oxyrhynchus Greek witness for this logion.
- Greek policy: no extant Thomas Greek text is available; a retroversion remains hypothetical.
- Canonical controls: no local canonical Greek control has been established in the current files.
- Internal controls: broader Thomas themes of non-attachment, wandering, and outsider identity.

## Rationale

Logion 42 is striking because of its brevity: "Become passersby." That brevity makes it memorable, but it also makes evidence evaluation difficult. Without an extant Greek witness or a secure early control, the saying cannot carry publication-level confidence for the earliest reconstruction.

It may preserve an old itinerant or stranger motif, but at present it is better treated as a Thomas identity saying requiring wider comparative work.

## Appendix Use

Use the logion as a reader-facing example of why a short aphorism is not automatically early. The appendix should discuss possible interpretations: itinerancy, detachment, strangerhood, refusal of possession, and passage through the world.
