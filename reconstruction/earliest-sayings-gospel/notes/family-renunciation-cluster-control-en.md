# Family-Renunciation Cluster-Control Note v0.1

Status: cluster-control note, 2026-07-18.

Target logia: 16, 55, 99, 101.

## Purpose

This note controls a difficult cluster where early Jesus tradition, synoptic redaction, and Thomasine symbolic development overlap. The project must not remove hard sayings merely because they are uncomfortable, but it also must not print a later ascetic or post-Easter form as the earliest recoverable gospel text.

The practical question is whether the clean reader should expand beyond the two family-related cores already included:

- Logion 16: division / household conflict core.
- Logion 99: true-family core.

## Cluster Decision

Do not expand the clean reader in this pass.

Keep:

- Logion 16 division / household core: `INCLUDE_WITH_MARKER`.
- Logion 99 true-family core: `INCLUDE_WITH_MARKER`.

Keep appendix-only for now:

- Logion 55 full form: `UNCERTAIN`.
- Logion 101 full form: `UNCERTAIN`.

The family-renunciation material in Logia 55 and 101 is important and may preserve early discipleship tradition, but the extant forms are too entangled with cross-bearing / worthiness language and true-mother / life symbolism to print as clean earliest reconstruction without further split-unit work.

## External Controls

### Logion 16

Matthew 10:34-36 and Luke 12:51-53 strongly control the division / household-conflict core. Luke is especially close in the household-divided pattern. Matthew controls the "not peace" and sword language.

This supports an early conflict saying, but not the whole Thomas form. Thomas 16 combines several motifs: division, fire, sword, war, household conflict, and `monachos`. The more motifs are printed as one clean unit, the more the reconstruction risks reproducing Thomas's composite or redactional form rather than the earliest recoverable saying.

### Logion 55

Luke 14:26-27 is the closest control for hate-family language joined to carrying the cross. Matthew 10:37-38 softens the family language into loving family more than Jesus and retains worthiness / cross-bearing language.

This means the tradition is not marginal. It belongs to the broader radical discipleship stream. The problem is its form. "Carry the cross" and "worthy of me" may reflect post-Easter discipleship language or later community self-understanding. The clean reader should therefore avoid printing the full Logion 55 as earliest reconstruction.

### Logion 99

Mark 3:31-35, Matthew 12:46-50, and Luke 8:19-21 strongly control the true-family core. The closest reconstructionally useful element is:

> Those who do the Father's will are my brothers and my mother.

The family-outside frame may be traditional narrative setting, and Thomas's kingdom-of-Father ending is likely a secondary or collectional conclusion. The clean reader should print only the core.

### Logion 101

Luke 14:26 and Matthew 10:37 control the family-renunciation opening, but Thomas 101 combines it with a second movement: bodily mother versus true mother who gives life. That ending fits Thomasine symbolic anthropology more naturally than a securely recoverable early sayings core.

The logion should remain appendix-only until a later split-unit pass can decide whether a shorter family-renunciation core can be responsibly reconstructed.

## Theological / Reader-Facing Distinctions

This cluster contains at least four different ideas:

1. Jesus' message creates social conflict, even inside the household.
2. True kinship is redefined around doing God's will.
3. Discipleship can demand a rupture with ordinary family priority.
4. Later communities may intensify this into ascetic identity, cross-bearing imitation, or symbolic rebirth language.

The clean reader should preserve the first two with markers because they have strong controls and manageable reconstructed cores. The appendix should preserve and explain the latter two because they are historically important, but not yet secure as clean earliest text.

## Reconstruction Implication

No change to the clean reader.

The family-related material already present in the clean reader is sufficient for this stage:

- Logion 16 represents social division / household conflict.
- Logion 99 represents redefined kinship.

Logia 55 and 101 remain essential for the appendix because they show how radical discipleship and family renunciation developed or circulated, but they should not be promoted without a separate split-core decision.

## Evidence Dossier Use

In the publication dossier, this cluster should be used as a methodological example:

- The project does not sanitize difficult material.
- The project also does not overprint late or composite forms as earliest text.
- A saying may be historically important and still remain outside the clean reader.
- Appendix-only is not rejection; it is controlled placement.
