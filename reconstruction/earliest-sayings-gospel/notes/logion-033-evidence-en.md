Status: evidence note v0.1.

## Working Units

Unit A:

> What you hear in your ear, proclaim from your rooftops.

Unit B:

> No one lights a lamp and puts it under a basket or in a hidden place; it is put on a lampstand so that its light may be seen.

## Main Reason

Logion 33 is a composite but promising unit. It combines two sayings with strong synoptic controls:

- ear/rooftop proclamation: Matthew 10:27 and Luke 12:3;
- lamp/lampstand visibility: Mark 4:21, Matthew 5:15, Luke 8:16, and Luke 11:33.

P.Oxy. 1 preserves only the opening of Logion 33. Therefore the ear/rooftop unit has partial Greek Thomas support, while the lamp unit has complete Coptic Thomas plus strong synoptic controls but no extant Greek Thomas witness in P.Oxy. 1.

## Greek Retroversion Note

For the lamp unit, any Greek form in the reconstruction must be marked:

`Greek retroversion, hypothetical`

The likely Greek shape can be controlled by the synoptic lamp tradition, but it is not an extant Thomas Greek witness.

## Reconstruction Implication

Add Logion 33 to the Ukrainian reader with a split-unit marker. Keep both subunits marked because the extant Thomas form is composite and the Greek witness is only partial.
