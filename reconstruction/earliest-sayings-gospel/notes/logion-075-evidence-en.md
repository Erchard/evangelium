# Logion 75 Evidence Note

Status: evidence rationale v0.1.

## Decision

`DEFER`; keep outside the clean reader.

## Working Unit

> Many stand at the door, but the monachoi enter the bridal chamber.

## Textual Witnesses

- Coptic base: extant NHC II,2; primary preserved witness.
- P.Oxy. 1: not preserved.
- P.Oxy. 654: not preserved.
- P.Oxy. 655: not preserved.
- Canonical control: Matthew 25:1-13 is a thematic bridal/door control, not a Thomas witness.
- Cluster control: `thomas-unity-monachos-cluster-en.md`.

## Main Evidence

The saying is compact and important for Thomasine identity language. Its key terms, especially `monachos` and bridal chamber imagery, belong to the same cluster as Logia 22, 23, 49, and 106.

## Reason For Non-Inclusion

The logion lacks a loaded Greek Thomas witness and its distinctive language may be a Thomasine theological marker. Its value for interpreting Thomas is high; its value for the earliest reconstructed sayings gospel remains unproven.

## Reconstruction Implication

Do not print Logion 75 in the clean reader. Use it as appendix evidence for Thomasine monachos / bridal chamber theology.

## Remaining Work

Develop a fuller monachos/bridal chamber study. Translation should avoid flattening `monachos` into only "solitary"; possible senses include solitary, single, unified, or spiritually whole.
