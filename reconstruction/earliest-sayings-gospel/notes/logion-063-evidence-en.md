## Main Evidence

Thomas 63 and Luke 12:16-21 share the same basic narrative architecture:

- a rich man has abundance;
- he plans agricultural production and storage;
- he imagines future security or absence of need;
- death arrives suddenly;
- wealth-security is exposed as unreliable.

The Thomas form is shorter and less explicitly moralized than Luke. Luke includes divine address and an explicit moral conclusion about storing treasure for oneself rather than being rich toward God. The clean reconstruction does not import those Lukan expansions into the reader text.

## Early-Core Assessment

The included early-core candidate is the short story:

> A rich man plans future sufficiency through stored goods, but dies that night.

This core is vivid, parabolic, and plausible as a mobile anti-wealth or wealth-security warning. It can function without Luke's explicit moral conclusion.
