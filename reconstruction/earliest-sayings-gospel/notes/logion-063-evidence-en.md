## Main Evidence

Thomas 63 and Luke 12 share the same basic plot:

- a rich man has abundance;
- he plans storage and future security;
- death arrives suddenly;
- the planned security is exposed as foolish.

Thomas appears shorter and less explicitly moralized than Luke, though this needs a full text comparison.

## Early-Core Assessment

Possible early core:

> A rich man planned to store his goods and live without need, but he died that night.

This core is vivid, parabolic, and plausible as a short warning against wealth-security.

## Secondary-Layer Indicators

The full form may have been shaped in relation to anti-wealth teaching. Luke's version contains a direct divine speech and moral conclusion; Thomas may omit these or preserve a shorter version.
