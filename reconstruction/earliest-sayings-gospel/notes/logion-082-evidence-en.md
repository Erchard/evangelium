## Source Basis

- Coptic base: extant in NHC II,2.

- Greek policy: hypothetical retroversion only.
- Internal control: Logion 10 for fire imagery.
- Canonical/thematic control: Luke 12:49 for fire on the earth.

## Rationale

The aphorism is vivid: nearness to Jesus is nearness to fire, and distance from Jesus is distance from the kingdom. It may preserve early eschatological or charismatic fire language, but present support is mostly thematic.

It should be deferred to a fire/kingdom cluster rather than printed as a clean early saying.

## Appendix Use

Use it to explain how Thomas links proximity to Jesus with danger, purification, judgment, or transformative fire.
