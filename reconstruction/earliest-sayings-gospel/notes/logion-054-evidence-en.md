## Main Evidence

Thomas 54 is extremely short:

> Blessed are the poor, for yours is the kingdom of heaven.

Luke has the closest form with "poor" and "yours"; Matthew has "poor in spirit" and "kingdom of heaven." Thomas therefore stands between the Lukan social form and the Matthean "kingdom of heaven" language.

## Early-Core Assessment

Possible early core:

> Blessed are the poor, for yours is the kingdom.

This is one of the strongest beatitude candidates in Thomas: short, memorable, and strongly controlled.

## Secondary-Layer Indicators

The phrase "kingdom of heaven" may reflect Matthean language, Coptic tradition, or a broader Jewish idiom. It prevents an unmarked inclusion.

## Limits

No extant Greek Thomas witness in the loaded corpus. Direction of dependence between Thomas, Luke, Matthew, and shared beatitude tradition remains unresolved.
