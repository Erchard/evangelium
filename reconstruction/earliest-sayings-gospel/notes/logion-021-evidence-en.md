# Logion 21 Evidence Rationale v0.1

## Decision

Current publication decision: `DEFER_TO_CLUSTER`.

Reader implication: do not include Logion 21 in the clean reconstruction as a full unit.

## Source Basis

- Coptic base: extant in NHC II,2.
- P.Oxy. status: no loaded Oxyrhynchus Greek witness for this logion.
- Greek policy: any Greek form must be treated as hypothetical unless based on a canonical control subunit.
- Canonical controls: Matthew 24:43 and Luke 12:39 for the thief/householder motif; Mark 13:33-37 for watchfulness; Mark 4:29 for the harvest/sickle motif.
- Internal controls: Logion 37 for undressing/shame imagery; broader Thomas watchfulness and knowledge language.

## Rationale

Logion 21 is highly composite. It contains a dialogue with Mary, children on another's field, an undressing image, a thief/householder warning, a command to watch against the world, and a harvest image.

Several subunits have plausible early controls, especially the thief and harvest motifs. Yet the whole Thomas composition cannot be treated as a clean early saying. The project should avoid flattening this into a single reconstructed logion.

## Appendix Use

Use Logion 21 as a model for explaining composite material. The appendix should divide it into subunits and show that some elements may preserve early tradition while the full Thomas arrangement remains deferred.
