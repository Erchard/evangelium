# Wealth / Renunciation Cluster-Control

Status: cluster-control note v0.1, 2026-07-18.

## Purpose

This note controls the sayings about wealth, possessions, lending, inheritance, treasure, banquet exclusion, yoke/rest, and renunciation of the world. It explains why the clean reader includes some wealth-related cores while keeping other attractive units in the appendix.

No clean-reader membership changes are made by this note.

## Included Reader Representatives

### Logion 63

Logion 63 is the clean reader's main wealth-warning parable. It is printed only as a short rich-fool core because Luke 12:16-21 supplies close narrative control: abundance, storage planning, imagined security, and sudden death. The marker remains necessary because direction of dependence is unresolved and no loaded extant Greek Thomas witness preserves the logion.

### Logion 72

Logion 72 gives the refusal to arbitrate an inheritance dispute. Luke 12:13-15 is the main control. The clean reader prints the compact refusal, not Luke's full surrounding warning about greed. This keeps the reconstructed unit narrow and avoids importing the canonical setting.

### Logion 95

Logion 95 combines non-interest lending and giving without expecting repayment. Luke 6:34-35 controls the non-repayment motif, while Jewish legal and wisdom traditions control the anti-usury background. It remains marked because the preserved Thomas form may combine more than one ethical strand.

### Logion 100

Logion 100 includes the Caesar/God tribute core with strong Mark/Matthew/Luke control. The Thomas-specific addition, \"what is mine, give to me,\" remains outside the clean reader because it looks like a later or community-specific interpretive clause.

## Appendix-Only Or Deferred Units

### Logion 64

The banquet-invitation core has serious early-tradition pressure through Luke 14:16-24 and Matthew 22:1-14. Yet the Thomas form is long, dependence-risky, and ends with an anti-merchant exclusion. The ending sharpens the parable into a community boundary against commercial life. The whole unit therefore remains appendix-only.

### Logion 76

The pearl and imperishable-treasure motifs are both controlled, mainly by Matthew 13:45-46, Matthew 6:19-20, and Luke 12:33. The problem is composition: Thomas links pearl, merchant, treasure, moth, and worm material in a way that should be split before any reader decision. It remains appendix-only.

### Logion 81

Logion 81 is short and memorable: the rich should reign, and the powerful should renounce. It may preserve reversal wisdom, irony, or Thomasine renunciation rhetoric. But it lacks a loaded extant Greek Thomas witness and lacks close external wording control. It remains `DEFER_TO_CLUSTER` / appendix-only.

### Logion 90

Logion 90 has a strong but narrow Matthew 11:28-30 control. That very closeness creates the main problem: the project cannot tell whether Thomas preserves an independent rest/yoke saying, a Matthean-dependent form, or a later shared devotional formula. It remains appendix-only.

### Logion 109

Logion 109 has an attractive hidden-treasure core, but Matthew 13:44 is the main close external control and the Thomas form is heavily expanded through inheritance, sale, discovery, and lending at interest. This makes the full form too risky for the clean reader.

### Logion 110

Logion 110 says that whoever has found the world and become rich should renounce the world. It belongs primarily to Thomasine world-renunciation language and has no close synoptic wording control. It remains appendix-only and should be discussed beside Logia 56, 80, 81, and 111.

## Methodological Rule

The clean reader should include only narrow, controlled wealth-related cores. It should not include every saying that sounds early, radical, anti-wealth, or spiritually attractive.

This cluster therefore uses a representative strategy:

- print the best-controlled short cores;
- mark dependence or textual uncertainty;
- keep composite, Matthew-only, heavily expanded, or internally Thomasine units in the appendix;
- use the appendix to let readers inspect the excluded material rather than hiding it.

## Dossier Implication

The evidence dossier should use this cluster as a model case for responsible selectivity. The reconstruction is not anti-wealth sayings collected loosely; it is a controlled reader that distinguishes:

- early parable or aphorism cores;
- canonical dependence risk;
- Thomasine community expansions;
- later spiritualizing or anti-world developments.
