# Logion 40 Evidence Rationale v0.1

## Decision

Current publication decision: `APPENDIX_ONLY_UNCERTAIN`.

Reader implication: do not include Logion 40 in the clean reconstruction in the current pass.

## Source Basis

- Coptic base: extant in NHC II,2.
- P.Oxy. status: no loaded Oxyrhynchus Greek witness for this logion.
- Greek policy: the Thomas Greek wording is not extant; any Greek Thomas form is hypothetical.
- Canonical control: Matthew 15:13, "Every plant that my heavenly Father did not plant will be uprooted."
- Thomas distinctive: the Coptic text speaks of a vine/plant planted outside the Father and lacking firmness.

## Rationale

The logion has a strong canonical control, which makes it important. But the direction of dependence remains unresolved: Thomas may preserve an independent compressed version, may adapt Matthew, or may reshape a shared plant-not-Father tradition.

Because the Thomas form is brief and not independently supported by a Greek Thomas witness, it is not yet safe as a clean-reader inclusion. It should remain appendix-only until the Matthew 15:13 relationship is treated in a dedicated control note.

## Appendix Use

Use Logion 40 to show how the project treats close canonical parallels. The appendix should present Matthew 15:13 in Greek, the Coptic Thomas form, and a concise discussion of possible dependence or shared tradition.
