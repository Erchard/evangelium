## Reconstructed Early Core

English:

> Do not seek the kingdom as a place in heaven or in the sea. The kingdom is within you and outside you.

Ukrainian:

> Не шукайте царство як місце в небі або в морі: царство всередині вас і зовні вас.

## Marked Expansion

English:

> When you know yourselves, you will be known, and you will know that you are children of the living Father. If you do not know yourselves, you are in poverty, and you are poverty.

Ukrainian:

> Коли пізнаєте себе, будете пізнані і зрозумієте, що ви діти живого Отця. Якщо ж не пізнаєте себе, ви перебуваєте в убогості і самі є убогістю.

## Coptic Witness

Source: `sources/primary_texts/coptic/logia-001-007-coptic-working.md`.

The Coptic witness preserves the full saying, including the external-location contrast, the inside/outside kingdom statement, and the self-knowledge continuation.

## Greek Witness

Source: `sources/primary_texts/greek_poxy/poxy654-transcription-working.md` and `sources/primary_texts/greek_poxy/dclp-poxy654-62840.xml`.

P.Oxy. 654, lines 9-21, preserves substantial parts of the logion but with many lacunae. Lines 15-16 preserve the key kingdom statement with important variants: kingdom of God, kingdom of the Father, or kingdom of heaven.

## Synoptic Parallels

Luke 17:20-21 is the closest thematic control because it also resists locating the kingdom by outward observation. Matthew 24:23 and Luke 17:23 are useful controls for "here / there" claims, but no direct literary dependence has been established.

Detailed synoptic table: `controls/synoptic-parallels/logion-003-luke-17-kingdom.md`.

The new comparison strengthens the case for a shared kingdom motif but does not prove direct dependence. The strongest contact is `ἐντὸς ὑμῶν`, shared by Luke 17:21 and P.Oxy. 654. Thomas, however, adds the heaven/sea contrast, the "outside you" phrase, and the self-knowledge continuation.

## Rationale

Reasons for inclusion:

- The contrast between external location and immediate presence is compact and memorable.
- The core kingdom motif has meaningful early-gospel comparanda without simply reproducing Luke.
- P.Oxy. 654 confirms a Greek form of the saying before the extant Coptic manuscript.

Reasons for marking the expansion:

- The self-knowledge / poverty sequence is strongly at home in Thomasine theological idiom.
- The Greek witness has major lacunae in the second half.
- The wording of "kingdom" itself is textually unstable in the Greek apparatus.

## Probability Assessment

| Period | Probability | Reason |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Before 60 CE | 15% | A short anti-location kingdom saying could be early, but the extant form is developed. |
| 60-90 CE | 30% | Plausible as a shared early kingdom tradition or independent sayings stream. |
| 90-130 CE | 40% | Strongly plausible for Thomasine development around kingdom and self-knowledge. |
| After 130 CE | 15% | Possible for final shaping, but less likely for the whole unit because of P.Oxy. 654. |
