## Working Core

> Do not be anxious from morning until evening and from evening until morning about what you will wear.

## Main Reason

Logion 36 has a strong but textually layered evidence profile:

- complete Coptic Thomas;
- Greek P.Oxy. 655 witness, though heavily restored in several lines;
- strong synoptic controls in Matthew 6:25-34 and Luke 12:22-31;
- P.Oxy. 5575 as an early control for the circulation of related Matthew/Luke/Thomas-like material in a mixed sayings environment.

The Coptic Thomas form is notably short and focused on clothing. P.Oxy. 655 preserves a fuller Greek form that includes food, clothing, lilies, spinning, and adding to one's age or stature. Matthew and Luke also preserve a broader anti-anxiety complex. P.Oxy. 5575 confirms that this broader complex circulated in an early Greek sayings environment, but it is not a Thomas manuscript and should not be used as a direct replacement for the Thomas text.

## Reconstruction Judgment

The safest reconstructable unit for the reader is the short anti-anxiety / clothing core. The fuller food/lilies/stature complex should be documented in the apparatus rather than printed as the main Thomas reconstruction.

## Reconstruction Implication

Add Logion 36 to the Ukrainian reader with a marker. The marker should indicate that the Coptic text is short, while P.Oxy. 655 and the synoptic controls preserve or imply a broader related complex.
