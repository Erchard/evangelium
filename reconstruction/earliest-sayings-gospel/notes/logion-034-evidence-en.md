# Logion 34 Evidence Note

Status: evidence note v0.1.

## Decision

`INCLUDE_WITH_MARKER`

## Working Core

> If a blind person leads a blind person, both fall into a pit.

## Main Reason

Logion 34 is short, complete in Coptic, and strongly controlled by Matthew 15:14 and Luke 6:39. The saying is a compact proverb about failed guidance. Thomas preserves it without the wider Matthean polemic against Pharisees.

No loaded Oxyrhynchus Greek Thomas witness preserves Logion 34. However, a Greek form is historically likely because the saying is securely attested in Greek synoptic tradition. Any Greek form in the reconstruction should be labeled `Greek retroversion, hypothetical`.

## Reconstruction Judgment

The short proverb is strong enough for marked inclusion. The marker remains necessary because the Thomas witness is Coptic-only in the loaded project sources and the direction of dependence remains unresolved.

## Reconstruction Implication

Add Logion 34 to the Ukrainian reader with a marker.
