## Main Evidence

Thomas 20 preserves a compact mustard-seed parable:

> The kingdom of heaven is like a mustard seed: small among seeds, but on worked earth it produces a large branch and becomes shelter for the birds of heaven.

The same basic parable is strongly attested in Mark, Matthew, and Luke. The shared core is:

> The kingdom is like a mustard seed: it begins small, grows large, and gives a place to the birds of heaven.

Thomas has no separate interpretation of the parable. This is important, but it does not by itself prove priority, because the synoptic forms also preserve the mustard-seed saying without an extended allegorical interpretation.

## Relationship To Synoptic Forms

Thomas 20 shares:

- the kingdom comparison;
- the mustard seed;
- the contrast between small seed and large growth;
- the birds of heaven;
- the shelter / nesting function.

Thomas is closest to Mark in the branch motif: Mark has "large branches"; Thomas has a "large branch." Thomas is close to Matthew in the phrase "kingdom of heaven." Thomas is close to Luke in brevity, but Luke lacks the explicit "smallest seed" element.

Therefore, Thomas 20 cannot be securely assigned to dependence on any single synoptic form. The safest model is that Thomas 20 witnesses a mobile parable tradition also represented in the synoptic gospels.

## Early-Core Assessment

Possible early core:

> The kingdom is like a mustard seed: it is small, but it grows and gives shelter to the birds of heaven.

This core is plausible as early parabolic material because it is short, memorable, agrarian, and multiply attested. The parable does not require later ecclesial allegory to function.

## Limits

The main limitation is the absence of an extant Greek witness for Thomas 20 in the loaded P.Oxy. corpus. The analysis depends on the Coptic NHC II,2 form and external synoptic comparison.

The Thomas form may be:

- an independent short form of the parable;
- a version of the same mobile tradition behind synoptic forms;
- a Thomasine condensation of a synoptic-like form;
- a later Coptic-preserved form of an older saying.

The present evidence cannot choose decisively among these.
