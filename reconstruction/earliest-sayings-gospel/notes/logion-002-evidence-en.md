# Logion 2 Evidence Note

Status: working evidence note v0.1.

## Decision

`INCLUDE_WITH_MARKER`

## Reconstructed Early Core

English:

> Let the one who seeks not stop seeking until they find.

Ukrainian:

> Нехай той, хто шукає, не припиняє шукати, доки не знайде.

## Marked Expansion

English:

> When they find, they will be disturbed; when disturbed, they will be amazed; and they will reign.

Ukrainian:

> Коли знайде, буде вражений; коли буде вражений, здивується; і царюватиме.

## Coptic Witness

Source: `sources/primary_texts/coptic/logia-001-007-coptic-working.md`.

The Coptic NHC II,2 form preserves the full saying and ends with "will reign over the All" (`ϥⲛⲁⲣ̅ ⲣ̅ⲣⲟ ⲉϫⲙ̅ ⲡⲧⲏⲣϥ`).

## Greek Witness

Source: `sources/primary_texts/greek_poxy/poxy654-transcription-working.md` and `sources/primary_texts/greek_poxy/dclp-poxy654-62840.xml`.

P.Oxy. 654, lines 5-9:

> [λέγει Ἰησοῦς·] μὴ παυσάσθω ὁ ζη[τῶν τοῦ ζητεῖν ἕως ἂν] εὕρη, καὶ ὅταν εὕρῃ [θαμβηθήσεται καὶ θαμ]βηθεὶς βασιλεύσει κα[ὶ βασιλεύσας ἀναπα]ήσεται.

The final "will rest" is reconstructed in the Greek witness and differs from the Coptic ending "will reign over the All." DCLP also records an Attridge variant `[ἐπαναπα]ήσεται`.

## Synoptic Parallels

Source: `controls/synoptic-parallels/logion-002-matthew-luke.md`.

Matthew 7:7 and Luke 11:9 preserve the formula:

> ask / seek / knock

The relevant shared element for Thomas 2 is:

> seek / find

Thomas 2 lacks "ask / receive" and "knock / be opened." This weakens a direct-copying model and supports either a shared traditional motif or an independent Thomasine development of the search/find saying.

## Rationale

Reasons for inclusion:

- The search/find core is short, aphoristic, and orally memorable.
- It has a real but limited synoptic parallel.
- Thomas does not reproduce the full Matthean/Lukan triad.
- P.Oxy. 654 confirms that the saying circulated in Greek before the extant Coptic manuscript.

Reasons for marking the expansion:

- The movement from finding to disturbance, amazement, reigning, and rest is more distinctively Thomasine.
- The Greek and Coptic endings diverge.
- The Greek rest-ending is reconstructed in a lacuna.
- The Coptic "reign over the All" may reflect a developed internal Thomasine idiom.
