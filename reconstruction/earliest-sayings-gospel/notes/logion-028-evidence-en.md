# Logion 28 Evidence Note

Status: evidence rationale v0.1.

## Decision

`UNCERTAIN`; keep outside the clean reader.

## Working Unit

> Jesus stands in the world, appears in flesh, finds people drunk rather than thirsty, and grieves over their blind hearts.

## Textual Witnesses

- Coptic base: extant NHC II,2; preserves the fuller form including the empty-world and sobering ending.
- P.Oxy. 1: extant but partial; preserves the opening and middle of the saying.
- P.Oxy. 654: not preserved.
- P.Oxy. 655: not preserved.
- Canonical controls: Mark 2:17 and Luke 5:31-32 are distant thematic controls; Johannine world/blindness motifs are also thematic, not Thomas witnesses.

## Main Evidence

P.Oxy. 1 gives real Greek support for a substantial unit: world, flesh, drunkenness, thirst, and blind heart. This makes Logion 28 important. Yet the Greek witness does not securely preserve the full Coptic ending, and the preserved material mixes potentially early wisdom imagery with a more developed christological/world frame.

## Reason For Non-Inclusion

The project cannot yet isolate a clean early core. The thirst/blindness imagery may be promising, but the full saying carries christological and world-renunciation features that may be secondary or Thomasine.

## Reconstruction Implication

Do not print the full Logion 28 in the clean reader. Keep it as a high-priority split-core candidate for later analysis.

## Remaining Work

Test whether a shorter thirst/drunkenness/blind-heart core can be separated from the "appeared in flesh" and empty-world frame. Any Greek reconstruction must remain tied to the extant P.Oxy. 1 limits.
