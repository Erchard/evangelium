# Logion 17 Evidence Rationale v0.1

## Decision

Current publication decision: `APPENDIX_ONLY_UNCERTAIN`.

Reader implication: do not include Logion 17 in the clean reconstruction in the current pass.

## Source Basis

- Coptic base: extant in NHC II,2 and used as the preserved Thomas witness.
- P.Oxy. status: no loaded Oxyrhynchus Greek witness for this logion.
- Greek policy: no extant Thomas Greek text is available; a Greek form would be a hypothetical retroversion only.
- Canonical/intertextual controls: Isaiah 64:4 and 1 Corinthians 2:9 are the main controls for the "eye has not seen / ear has not heard" tradition.
- Distinctive feature: the Thomas form adds the hand-touch motif, which may be important but is not enough by itself to establish independence or priority.

## Rationale

The logion is short and memorable, but its strongest visible control is a known scriptural/christian formula. The additional "hand has not touched" element may reflect an independent variant, an expanded oral form, or a Thomas redactional development. With no Greek Thomas witness and no direct synoptic control, the safest publication decision is appendix-only uncertainty.

The saying may still preserve early sapiential or apocalyptic language about an unseen gift, but the project goal requires stricter evidence before presenting it as part of the earliest recoverable gospel.

## Appendix Use

Use the logion to explain a case where beauty and antiquity of theme are not the same as reconstructive confidence. The appendix should compare Isaiah 64:4, 1 Corinthians 2:9, and the Thomas hand motif.
