# Logion 106 Evidence Note

Status: evidence rationale v0.1.

## Decision

`DEFER`; keep outside the clean reader.

## Working Unit

> Make the two one; become children of humanity; say to the mountain, move, and it will move.

## Textual Witnesses

- Coptic base: extant NHC II,2; primary preserved witness.
- P.Oxy. 1: not preserved.
- P.Oxy. 654: not preserved.
- P.Oxy. 655: not preserved.
- Greek Thomas layer: hypothetical retroversion only.
- Canonical controls: Matthew 17:20, Matthew 21:21, and Mark 11:23 for the mountain-moving motif.
- Cluster control: `thomas-unity-monachos-cluster-en.md`.

## Main Evidence

The mountain-moving element has strong canonical controls, but Thomas makes the condition "when you make the two one." This links the saying to Thomasine unity theology rather than to the synoptic faith/prayer setting.

## Reason For Non-Inclusion

The logion is probably composite or recontextualized. Its externally controlled mountain-moving motif may be early, but the Thomas form is governed by unity language that functions as a caution marker in this project.

## Reconstruction Implication

Do not print Logion 106 in the clean reader. Use it to explain how Thomas can reshape mobile Jesus tradition through its unity/monachos framework.

## Remaining Work

Study Logia 22, 48, and 106 together. A future pass may test whether the mountain-moving core has independent value apart from the unity condition.
