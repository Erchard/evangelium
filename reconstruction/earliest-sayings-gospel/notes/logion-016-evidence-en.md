# Logion 16 Evidence Note

Status: evidence note v0.2.

## Decision

Short division/household core: `INCLUDE_WITH_MARKER`.

Full Logion 16: `UNCERTAIN`.

## Working Early Core

Possible early core:

> I did not come to bring peace, but division; a household will be divided.

This core is plausible because it is strongly supported by Matthew 10:34-36 and Luke 12:51-53. The preserved Thomas form, however, is not a clean early form.

## Main Reason

Thomas 16 appears to combine several layers:

1. a traditional division / family-conflict saying;
2. fire / sword / war imagery;
3. a household division pattern especially close to Luke 12:52-53;
4. a final `monachos` / solitary closure, which belongs to the Thomas unity / monachos cluster.

The result is important evidence for a mobile tradition, but not secure enough for direct inclusion in the reconstruction.

## Synoptic Comparison

Detailed table: `controls/synoptic-parallels/logion-016-division-family.md`.

Luke is the closest structural control because both Luke and Thomas contain the five-in-one-house pattern and the three-against-two / two-against-three division. Matthew is important because Thomas includes the sword motif.

## Monachos Ending

The final line about standing as `monachos` should not be included in the reconstructed early core.

The earlier cluster check established the rule that unity / monachos language is a Thomasine theological marker unless independently supported. In Logion 16 the monachos ending has no synoptic support and functions as a Thomasine closure to an otherwise externally supported division saying.

Cluster note: `notes/thomas-unity-monachos-cluster-en.md`.

The earlier probability applies mainly to the division/family-conflict core. The extant Thomas composition is more likely a developed or harmonized form.

## Reconstruction Implication

Add Logion 16 to the main reader text only as a short marked core:

> I did not come to bring peace, but division; a household will be divided.

The fire / sword / war sequence and the monachos ending should remain in the apparatus unless a later study gives stronger evidence. No loaded extant Greek Thomas witness preserves Logion 16; any Greek form must be labeled `Greek retroversion, hypothetical`.
