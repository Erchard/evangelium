## Main Evidence

Thomas 41 preserves the compact formula:

> Whoever has will be given; whoever does not have will lose even the little that he has.

The same aphoristic structure is widely attested in Mark, Matthew, and Luke. This broad attestation makes the saying one of the stronger candidates for early circulating tradition.

Thomas has two details that need attention:

- "in his hand";
- "the little that he has."

These may be independent variants, Coptic shaping, or a Thomasine sharpening of the paradox.

## Early-Core Assessment

Possible early core:

> Whoever has, more will be given; whoever does not have, even what he has will be taken.

The core is short, memorable, paradoxical, and multiply attested. It does not require a later ecclesial frame to function.

## Secondary-Layer Indicators

The main secondary risk is contextual: in the synoptic gospels the formula appears in interpretive or parabolic settings, while Thomas transmits it as a standalone saying. Standalone form may be early, but it may also be an extracted maxim.

## Limits

No loaded extant Greek Thomas witness preserves Logion 41. Direction of dependence cannot be resolved from the present evidence.
