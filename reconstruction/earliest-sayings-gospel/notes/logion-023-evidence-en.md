# Logion 23 Evidence Note

Status: evidence rationale v0.1.

## Decision

`UNCERTAIN`; keep outside the clean reader.

## Working Unit

> I shall choose one from a thousand and two from ten thousand, and they will stand as one.

## Textual Witnesses

- Coptic base: extant NHC II,2; primary preserved witness.
- P.Oxy. 1: not preserved in the loaded corpus.
- P.Oxy. 654: not preserved; this papyrus covers the prologue and Logia 1-7.
- P.Oxy. 655: not preserved in the loaded corpus.
- Canonical controls: no close canonical parallel has been established.
- Cluster control: `thomas-unity-monachos-cluster-en.md`.

## Main Evidence

The saying is short and memorable, but its strongest control is internal Thomasine language: election, solitary identity, and standing as one. The unity/monachos cluster shows that such language is important for Thomas, but internal repetition does not prove earliest recoverability.

## Reason For Non-Inclusion

The logion has no loaded Greek Thomas witness and no close external control. Its final "stand as one" phrase belongs directly to a Thomasine pattern that may express community identity or later theological shaping.

## Reconstruction Implication

Do not print Logion 23 in the clean reader. Use it in the appendix as a key witness to Thomasine election/unity language.

## Remaining Work

Study Jewish and early Christian remnant/election language before any future promotion. A future change would require independent control for the short election core apart from Thomasine unity language.
