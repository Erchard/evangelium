# Logion 47: Incompatibility Sayings Controls

Status: split-core control table v0.1.

## Short Conclusion

Logion 47 is a chain of incompatibility sayings. Several subunits are likely traditional, but the chain as a whole is secondary or collectional. Do not promote the full logion.

## Subunits

### 47A. Two Horses / Two Bows

Thomas-only opening:

> No one can ride two horses or draw two bows.

Assessment: useful thematic lead-in, but no strong loaded external control. Keep appendix-only for now.

Decision: `KEEP_APPENDIX_ONLY_FOR_NOW`.

### 47B. Two Masters

Thomas core:

> No servant can serve two masters; he will honor one and despise the other.

Controls:

- Matthew 6:24
- Luke 16:13

Assessment: strong, compact, aphoristic. This is the strongest subunit in Logion 47.

Decision: `PROMOTE_CANDIDATE_FOR_READER_PASS`, medium confidence.

### 47C. New Wine and Wineskins

Thomas core:

> New wine is not put into old wineskins.

Controls:

- Mark 2:22
- Matthew 9:17
- Luke 5:37-38

Assessment: strong control, but tightly embedded in synoptic controversy/new-old contexts. The Thomas chain adds old/new inversions and should not be printed whole.

Decision: `SPLIT_CORE_REVIEW`; possible candidate, lower than 47B.

### 47D. Patch

Thomas core:

> An old patch is not sewn onto a new garment, because a tear will result.

Controls:

- Mark 2:21
- Matthew 9:16
- Luke 5:36

Assessment: strong control but text direction and old/new orientation vary across traditions.

Decision: `KEEP_APPENDIX_ONLY_FOR_NOW`.

## Greek Status

No loaded extant Greek Thomas witness. Any Greek form must be marked `Greek retroversion, hypothetical`.

## Next Work

For the next clean-reader decision pass, consider only 47B as a possible addition. Keep the wine/patch units in appendix unless the project chooses to include a broader new/old incompatibility cluster.
