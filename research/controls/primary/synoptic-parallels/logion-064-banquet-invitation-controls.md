# Logion 64: Banquet Invitation Controls

Status: split-core control table v0.1.

## Short Conclusion

Logion 64 preserves a banquet-invitation pattern with close control in Luke 14:16-24 and broader control in Matthew 22:1-14. The banquet core is important, but the Thomas form is long and the final anti-merchant saying should not be promoted with the parable.

## Subunits

### 64A. Banquet Invitation and Refusals

Thomas core:

> A host prepared a dinner, invited guests, and they refused because of business, property, marriage, or village obligations. Others were brought in from outside.

Controls:

- Luke 14:16-24: closest banquet/refusal/invite-others form.
- Matthew 22:1-14: related but more allegorical wedding-banquet form.

Assessment: strong parable tradition, but the Thomas form may be a shortened or rearranged version of a Lukan-like story.

Decision: `PROMOTE_CANDIDATE_FOR_READER_PASS`, low-medium to medium confidence.

### 64B. Merchants and Traders Exclusion

Thomas ending:

> Buyers and merchants will not enter the places of my Father.

Assessment: likely Thomasine or secondary application. It sharpens the parable into anti-commercial exclusion and should remain outside the clean reader.

Decision: `KEEP_APPENDIX_ONLY_FOR_NOW`.

## Greek Status

No loaded extant Greek Thomas witness. Any Greek form must be marked `Greek retroversion, hypothetical`.

## Next Work

If promoted later, include only the banquet/refusal core and keep the anti-merchant ending in apparatus. The final decision must compare this unit with Logion 63 and the broader anti-wealth cluster.
