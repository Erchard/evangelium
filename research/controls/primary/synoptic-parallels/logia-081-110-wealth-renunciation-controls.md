## Logion 81 Control

Relevant files:

- `corpus/cards/logion-081.md`
- `reconstruction/earliest-sayings-gospel/notes/logion-081-evidence-en.md`

Logion 81 says that the one who has become rich should reign, and the one who has power should renounce. It is short and memorable, but its point is not transparent.

Main interpretive options:

1. Ironic reversal: wealth and power are exposed as unstable or spiritually dangerous.
2. Eschatological reversal: the rich/ruling figure is revalued in a kingdom context.
3. Thomasine renunciation: power is meaningful only when relinquished.
4. Composite aphorism: the two halves may have been joined because wealth, reign, power, and renunciation sounded thematically related.

Control decision:

- Logion 81 has no loaded extant Greek Thomas witness.
- Luke 12:16-21 controls Logion 63 strongly, but it does not directly control Logion 81's wording.
- Logion 110 is a strong internal parallel for wealth plus renunciation.
- The saying should remain appendix-only until the project can decide whether it is early reversal wisdom or Thomasine renunciation rhetoric.

Reconstruction implication: do not add Logion 81 to the clean reader. In the appendix, present it as a compact but unresolved wealth/power saying.

## Logion 110 Control

Relevant files:

- `corpus/cards/logion-110.md`
- `reconstruction/earliest-sayings-gospel/notes/logion-110-evidence-en.md`
- `reconstruction/earliest-sayings-gospel/notes/living-dead-world-cluster-control-en.md`

Logion 110 says that whoever has found the world and become rich should renounce the world. This is more explicitly anti-world than Logion 81 and therefore more strongly tied to Thomasine anthropology.

Main controls:

- Logion 81: wealth and renunciation.
- Logion 63: anti-wealth story core, externally controlled through Luke 12:16-21.
- Logia 56, 80, 111: world/body/world-unworthy cluster.
- Logion 27: fasting from the world as a related Thomasine metaphor, though not a direct control.

Control decision:

- Logion 110 has no loaded extant Greek Thomas witness.
- It has no close synoptic wording control.
- Its world-renunciation language belongs primarily to Thomasine anti-world discourse.
- It is useful for understanding Thomas, but weak for reconstructing the earliest recoverable sayings gospel.

Reconstruction implication: keep Logion 110 appendix-only. Any Greek form must remain `Greek retroversion, hypothetical`.
