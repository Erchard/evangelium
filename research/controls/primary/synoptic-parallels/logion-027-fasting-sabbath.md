## Early-Christian Ritual Controls

| Source | Motif | Comparison |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Thomas 6 | question about fasting, prayer, almsgiving, food | Shows Thomas places ritual questions inside ethical/revelatory frames. |
| Thomas 14 | fasting/prayer/almsgiving critique; mission and food | Shows a sharper critique of ritual acts. |
| Thomas 104 | prayer/fasting and bridegroom | Closest internal Thomas control for synoptic fasting tradition. |
| Didache 8 | regulated fasting days and prayer | Contrasts with Thomas: communal prescription rather than symbolic world-renunciation. |

## Assessment

Thomas 27 is strongly attested as a transmitted saying, especially because P.Oxy. 1 preserves the whole Greek form and P.Oxy. 5575 gives an additional early control for related material.

However, the synoptic controls are only thematic. They show that fasting and Sabbath traditions were active in early Jesus tradition, but they do not independently confirm Thomas 27's exact symbolic formulas.

## Reconstruction Implication

Decision: `UNCERTAIN`.

Do not add Thomas 27 to the main reconstructed reader text at this stage. It should remain an important apparatus and dossier item for the ritual-ethics cluster and for the circulation of Thomas-like sayings in Greek.
