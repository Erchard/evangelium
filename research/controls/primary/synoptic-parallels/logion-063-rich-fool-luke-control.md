## Thomas Core

Current reader core:

> A rich man had much property. He planned to sow, reap, plant, and fill his storehouses so that he would lack nothing. That night he died.

This core excludes any hearing formula and does not import Luke's divine address or explicit moral conclusion.

## Luke Control

Luke 12:16-21 has the same narrative architecture:

- rich man's abundance;
- planning storage;
- future security;
- sudden death;
- exposure of wealth-security as folly.

Luke adds divine address and an explicit moral conclusion about storing treasure for oneself rather than being rich toward God. Thomas has a shorter narrative and a less explicit interpretation.

## Direction of Dependence

The evidence still permits several models:

1. Thomas preserves a shorter parable core and Luke expands it.
2. Thomas extracts and abbreviates Luke's rich-fool parable.
3. Both depend on a mobile anti-wealth parable tradition.

The current evidence cannot decide direction securely. The shorter Thomas form supports marked inclusion, but not unmarked certainty or Thomasine priority.
