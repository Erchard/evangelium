# Logia 24, 28, 29, 30, 77: Papyrus-Boundary Controls

Status: technical control package v0.1, 2026-07-18.

## Purpose

This control package resolves the highest-priority `CREATE_CONTROL_FILE` group from `project/synoptic-control-gap-classification-2026-07-18.md`.

The controlled issue is not ordinary synoptic parallelism. The main question is how to use partial Greek papyrus witnesses without overstating them:

- P.Oxy. 655 and the uncertain Thomas 24(?) catalogue relation.
- P.Oxy. 1 and the partial Logion 28 witness.
- P.Oxy. 1 and the tiny Logion 29 poverty overlap.
- P.Oxy. 1 and the unstable Logion 30 / 77b sequence boundary.

No clean-reader membership changes are made by this pass.

## Global Rule

Use `extant Greek witness` only for the exact material actually preserved in the loaded papyrus evidence. Use `Greek retroversion, hypothetical` for any Greek wording reconstructed from Coptic or canonical controls beyond the extant fragment.

Do not treat a catalogue-level association, a heavily damaged line, or a neighboring papyrus sequence as proof for the full Coptic logion.

## Logion 24 Control

Relevant local files:

- `corpus/cards/logion-024.md`
- `reconstruction/earliest-sayings-gospel/notes/logion-024-evidence-en.md`
- `sources/primary_texts/greek_poxy/dclp-poxy655-62839.xml`

P.Oxy. 655 is important because the local source inventory records it as Thomas 24(?), 36-39. The question mark matters. In the current local extraction, the extant Greek line-level material around column i lines 17-23 is used in the project as Logion 37 appearance/undressing material, not as a Greek witness to the Coptic light/person-of-light saying in Logion 24.

The Coptic Logion 24 is a coherent light/location saying: the disciples ask about Jesus' place, and the answer speaks of light in a person of light. The P.Oxy. 655 material does not preserve that Coptic light saying in a card-ready form.

Control decision:

- P.Oxy. 655 may be cited as an uncertain catalogue-level association with Thomas 24(?).
- It must not be cited as direct Greek support for the Coptic light/person-of-light wording.
- Any Greek reconstruction of the Coptic light saying must be marked `Greek retroversion, hypothetical`.

Reconstruction implication: Logion 24 remains outside the clean reader. The appendix should explain why "possible Greek papyrus association" is not the same as "secure Greek witness."

## Logion 28 Control

Relevant local files:

- `corpus/cards/logion-028.md`
- `reconstruction/earliest-sayings-gospel/notes/logion-028-evidence-en.md`
- `sources/primary_texts/greek_poxy/dclp-poxy1-62838.xml`

P.Oxy. 1 gives real Greek support for a substantial part of Logion 28. The card records the Greek witness as preserving the core sequence:

> ἔ[σ]την ἐν μέσῳ τοῦ κόσμου καὶ ἐν σαρκὶ ὤφθην αὐτοῖς· καὶ εὗρον πάντας μεθύοντας καὶ οὐδένα εὗρον διψῶντα ἐν αὐτοῖς· καὶ πονεῖ ἡ ψυχή μου ... ὅτι τυφλοί εἰσιν τῇ καρδίᾳ ...

This is direct papyrus support for the world/flesh/drunkenness/thirst/blind-heart material. It is not direct support for the entire Coptic ending, especially the fuller material about coming into the world empty, leaving empty, and later sobering.

Control decision:

- The extant Greek witness supports the opening/middle of the logion.
- The final Coptic movement must remain Coptic-based unless future Greek evidence is added.
- A shorter thirst/drunkenness/blind-heart unit can be tested later, but it should not be printed now because it remains entangled with the christological "appeared in flesh" and world frame.

Reconstruction implication: Logion 28 remains outside the clean reader as `UNCERTAIN`; it is a high-value split-core candidate, not a current reader unit.

## Logion 29 Control

Relevant local files:

- `corpus/cards/logion-029.md`
- `reconstruction/earliest-sayings-gospel/notes/logion-029-evidence-en.md`
- `sources/primary_texts/greek_poxy/dclp-poxy1-62838.xml`

P.Oxy. 1 gives only minimal Greek control for Logion 29. The local card records the surviving overlap as the final poverty phrase:

> [...] [ἐνοι]κ[εῖ] [ταύτ]η[ν τ]ὴν πτωχείαν

The Coptic form contains flesh/spirit/body language using transparent Greek loan terminology (`ⲥⲁⲣⲝ`, `ⲡⲛ̅ⲁ̅`, `ⲥⲱⲙⲁ`), but transparent Greek vocabulary in Coptic is not the same as extant Greek manuscript control for the whole saying.

Control decision:

- P.Oxy. 1 directly controls only the tiny poverty overlap.
- The full flesh/spirit/body argument remains Coptic-based.
- Greek reconstruction of the full unit must be labeled `Greek retroversion, hypothetical`.

Reconstruction implication: Logion 29 remains appendix-only. The final wealth-in-poverty aphorism may be evaluated separately later, but the full anthropological reflection should not enter the clean reader.

## Logion 30 / 77b Sequence Control

Relevant local files:

- `corpus/cards/logion-030.md`
- `corpus/cards/logion-077.md`
- `reconstruction/earliest-sayings-gospel/notes/logion-030-evidence-en.md`
- `reconstruction/earliest-sayings-gospel/notes/logion-077-evidence-en.md`
- `sources/primary_texts/greek_poxy/dclp-poxy1-62838.xml`

P.Oxy. 1 creates a genuine but difficult control problem. The local cards record a Greek sequence containing damaged Logion 30 material followed immediately by the stone/wood unit:

> ... ὅπου ἐὰν ὦσιν [τρ]ε[ῖς] ... ἄθεοι ... ἐστιν μόνος ... ἐγώ εἰμι μετ' αὐτοῦ· ἔγειρον τὸν λίθον κἀκεῖ εὑρήσεις με· σχίσον τὸ ξύλον κἀγὼ ἐκεῖ εἰμι

The Coptic tradition treats the stone/wood unit as the second part of Logion 77. P.Oxy. 1 places it immediately after the damaged Logion 30 material. This means the Greek papyrus is real evidence, but not a clean witness to the later Coptic logion boundaries.

Control decision for Logion 30:

- P.Oxy. 1 is direct Greek evidence for a damaged presence/one-two-three unit.
- The Greek `ἄθεοι` problem and the Coptic "three gods / they are gods" form do not align cleanly.
- The presence core may be early or useful, but it is not stable enough for clean-reader inclusion.

Control decision for Logion 77:

- P.Oxy. 1 directly supports the 77b stone/wood presence unit.
- P.Oxy. 1 does not support the full Coptic "I am the light / I am the All" opening.
- Full Logion 77 must remain appendix-only; 77b can be treated later as a separate split-core candidate.

Reconstruction implication: Logia 30 and 77 remain outside the clean reader. Future work may test two separate recoverable cores:

- Logion 30 presence-with-one/two.
- Logion 77b stone/wood presence.

Those tests must not assume the full Coptic arrangement is original.
