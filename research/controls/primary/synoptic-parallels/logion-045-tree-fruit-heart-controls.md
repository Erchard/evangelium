# Logion 45: Tree/Fruit and Heart/Treasure Controls

Status: split-core control table v0.1.

## Short Conclusion

Logion 45 contains two strong traditional units:

1. grapes/thorns and figs/thistles;
2. good or evil person bringing good or evil from the heart's store.

Both have strong Matthean/Lukan controls, especially Luke 6:43-45, where tree/fruit and heart/speech are already joined. The full Thomas logion should not be promoted as one unit. The two subunits remain live candidates, but they need a later reader-pass decision because the Thomas form may be a conflation of already-related synoptic material.

## Thomas Units

### 45A. Grapes and Figs

Thomas core:

> Grapes are not gathered from thorns, nor figs from thistles.

Closest controls:

- Matthew 7:16: grapes are not gathered from thorns, nor figs from thistles.
- Luke 6:44: figs are not gathered from thorns, nor grapes from brambles.

Assessment: strong aphoristic control; possible early wisdom core.

### 45B. Good/Evil Treasure of the Heart

Thomas core:

> A good person brings forth good from the heart's treasure; an evil person brings forth evil; from the abundance of the heart one speaks.

Closest controls:

- Matthew 12:35: good and evil treasure.
- Luke 6:45: good and evil person brings from the heart's store; mouth speaks from heart's abundance.

Assessment: strong control, but more developed than 45A and closely aligned with Luke's joined complex.

## Decision
