# Logia 34-35: Bridge Synoptic Controls

Status: evidence table v0.1.

## Purpose

This table closes the small corpus gap between Logia 31-33 and the P.Oxy. 655 block 36-39.

## Witnesses and Controls

| Logion | Coptic Thomas | Extant Greek Thomas | Greek retroversion | Main controls | Assessment |
| ---: | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 34 | Complete | No loaded P.Oxy. witness | `Greek retroversion, hypothetical`, high confidence | Matthew 15:14; Luke 6:39 | Very strong short proverbial core: blind leading blind, both fall into a pit. |
| 35 | Complete | No loaded P.Oxy. witness | `Greek retroversion, hypothetical`, medium-high confidence | Mark 3:27; Matthew 12:29; Luke 11:21-22 | Strong short strong-man/house/binding core; Thomas is closest to Mark/Matthew shape. |

## Synoptic Controls

| Thomas unit | Main controls | Assessment |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 34 blind leading blind | Matthew 15:14; Luke 6:39 | Very strong. Matthew and Luke preserve the same compact image. Thomas is short and lacks the Matthean polemical frame. |
| 35 enter strong man's house / bind / plunder | Mark 3:27; Matthew 12:29 | Very strong. Mark and Matthew closely control the core structure: enter house, bind strong man, plunder/take goods. |
| 35 armed strong man / stronger attacker | Luke 11:21-22 | Important thematic control, but Luke's form is more developed and less close to Thomas than Mark/Matthew. |

## Reconstruction Implication

Both logia may be included with markers:

- 34 as a marked short proverb about blind guidance;
- 35 as a marked strong-man saying.

For the Greek layer, both should be labeled `Greek retroversion, hypothetical`, not extant Greek Thomas.
