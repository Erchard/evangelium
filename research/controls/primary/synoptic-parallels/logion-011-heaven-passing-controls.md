# Logion 11: Heaven-Passing and Composite Controls

Статус: синоптична evidence table v0.1.

## Short Decision

Logion 11: `UNCERTAIN`.

Reader status: do not add to the main reconstructed text yet.

Greek status: no loaded P.Oxy. witness preserves Thomas 11. Any Greek form must be labeled `Greek retroversion, hypothetical`.

## Synoptic Controls

| Source | Textual motif | Assessment |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Mark 13:31 | Heaven and earth will pass away; my words will not pass away | Strong control for heaven/earth passing formula. |
| Matthew 24:35 | Heaven and earth will pass away; my words will not pass away | Strong control, close to Mark. |
| Luke 21:33 | Heaven and earth will pass away; my words will not pass away | Strong control, close to Mark/Matthew. |
| Thomas 11 | This heaven and the one above it will pass away | Related but not identical; omits earth and the enduring words clause. |
| Thomas 111 | Heavens and earth roll up/pass before you; living from the Living One will not see death | Internal Thomas control; suggests a later or collection-level eschatological cluster. |

## Rationale

The heaven-passing motif is strongly controlled by the synoptic apocalyptic saying. However, Thomas 11 does not preserve the synoptic formula directly. It changes the structure from "heaven and earth" to "this heaven and the one above it" and omits the contrasting clause about Jesus' words not passing away.

The rest of the logion is even more distinctively Thomasine: dead/living paradox, eating what is dead, being in the light, and one/two anthropology. These motifs may be important for Thomas's theology, but they are not strong candidates for the earliest reconstructed reader text at this stage.

## Publication Implication

Keep Logion 11 out of the main Ukrainian reader text. Preserve it in the apparatus and evidence dossier as a composite `UNCERTAIN` unit.
