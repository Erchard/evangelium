# Logion 41: Has / Given Formula

Status: synoptic control table v0.1.

## Short Conclusion

Thomas 41 is a strong candidate for `INCLUDE_WITH_MARKER`. The saying is short, aphoristic, and multiply attested in Mark, Matthew, and Luke. The extant Thomas witness is Coptic only; no loaded Greek Thomas fragment preserves this logion.

## Reconstruction Decision

Decision: `INCLUDE_WITH_MARKER`.

Reason: the core formula is broadly attested and probably belongs to early mobile Jesus-saying tradition, but the exact Thomas wording must remain marked because the Greek Thomas form is not extant in the loaded witnesses.

## Thomas Text

Coptic Thomas 41:

> ⲡⲉϫⲉ ⲓ̅ⲥ̅ ϫⲉ ⲡⲉⲧⲉⲩⲛ̅ⲧⲁϥ` ϩⲛ̅ ⲧⲉϥ`ϭⲓϫ ⲥⲉⲛⲁϯⲛⲁϥ` ⲁⲩⲱ ⲡⲉⲧⲉ ⲙⲛ̅ⲧⲁϥ ⲡⲕⲉϣⲏⲙ ⲉⲧⲟⲩⲛ̅ⲧⲁϥ` ⲥⲉⲛⲁϥⲓⲧϥ̅ ⲛ̅ⲧⲟⲟⲧϥ`

Literal Ukrainian:

> Тому, хто має в руці, дадуть; а в того, хто не має, навіть мале, що має, заберуть у нього.

Greek Thomas status: no loaded extant Greek Thomas witness. Any Greek Thomas form is a hypothetical retroversion.

## Direction of Dependence

The formula is too widespread to assign Thomas 41 to direct dependence on one synoptic text. Thomas does not reproduce Mark, Matthew, or Luke exactly. The distinctive "in his hand" and "the little" may preserve a variant form or reflect later Thomas/Coptic shaping.

The safest model is shared mobile tradition with unresolved literary dependence.

## Possible Early Core

> Whoever has, more will be given; whoever does not have, even what he has will be taken.
