## Main Evidence

Thomas 109 is a developed hidden-treasure parable. Matthew 13:44 preserves a much shorter hidden-treasure-in-field parable. Thomas expands the story through inheritance, ignorance, sale, purchase, plowing, discovery, and lending money at interest.

The hidden-treasure core is strong, but the received Thomas form is substantially elaborated and internally tense with Logion 95's anti-interest stance.

## Early-Core Assessment

Possible early core:

> The kingdom is like treasure hidden in a field.

The rest of the Thomas narrative may be interpretive expansion around ignorance, discovery, ownership, and wealth.

## Secondary-Layer Indicators

The inheritance/sale sequence and the final usury motif look secondary. The lending-at-interest ending is especially difficult and may be ironic, critical, or simply later narrative development.

## Limits

No extant Greek Thomas witness is loaded. Matthew is the only close canonical control. Because the Thomas version is much longer than Matthew's, direction of dependence and redactional growth are major unresolved questions.

## Reconstruction Implication

Do not add in this pass. Only the hidden-treasure core could be considered later, and even that must be weighed against Matthew-only control and Thomas's long expansion.
