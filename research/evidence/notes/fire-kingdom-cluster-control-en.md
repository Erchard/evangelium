# Fire / Kingdom Cluster-Control Note v0.1

Status: cluster-control note, 2026-07-18.

Target logia: 10, 82.

## Purpose

This note controls whether the project's current fire material should remain represented by the short Logion 10 fire-casting core or expand to include Logion 82.

The controlling publication question is simple: does Logion 82 add a recoverable early saying, or does it mainly intensify Thomas's symbolic use of fire and proximity to Jesus?

## Cluster Decision

Do not expand the clean reader in this pass.

Keep:

- Logion 10 fire-casting core: `INCLUDE_WITH_MARKER`.

Keep in apparatus:

- Logion 10 watching / guarding continuation.
- Logion 82 full form.

Logion 82 is important for interpretation, but its support is mostly internal and thematic. It should be treated as appendix-only, not as a clean early reconstruction.

## External Control

Luke 12:49 is the primary external control for Logion 10:

> Fire I came to cast upon the earth.

This supports a compact fire-casting core. The wording is not identical: Luke has earth/land, while Thomas has world/cosmos. Luke continues with an already-kindled fire question, while Thomas continues with watching or guarding until the fire blazes.

The result is a strong case for a short core, but not for printing the whole Thomas form.

## Internal Thomas Controls

Thomas 16 combines fire with division, sword, war, and household conflict. That combination is already treated cautiously: only the division / household core is printed in the clean reader.

Thomas 82 links nearness to Jesus with nearness to fire and distance from Jesus with distance from the kingdom. This is powerful for interpretation, but it does not provide the same kind of external control as Luke 12:49 provides for Logion 10.

The internal evidence therefore works in two directions:

- it confirms that fire is meaningful in Thomas;
- it also raises the risk that Logion 82 is Thomasine symbolic intensification rather than an independently recoverable early saying.

## Kingdom-Language Control

The clean reader already has several kingdom units or cores: Logia 3, 20, 22, 54, 96, 99, and 107, with additional kingdom material in the appendix. Logion 82 does not need to be added merely to represent kingdom language.

Its distinctive contribution is proximity: closeness to Jesus means closeness to dangerous transformative fire; distance from Jesus means distance from the kingdom. That is interpretively important, but it is not enough for clean-reader promotion.

## Reconstruction Implication

No change to the clean reader.

The clean reader should continue to print:

> I cast fire upon the world.

The appendix should explain:

- why the Logion 10 continuation is left outside the clean text;
- why Logion 82 is preserved as serious interpretive material;
- why thematic connection to Luke 12:49 does not equal direct textual control for Logion 82;
- why any Greek form of either saying remains `Greek retroversion, hypothetical` unless an extant Greek witness is identified.

## Evidence Dossier Use

This cluster should support a methodological section on motif control. A shared motif can strengthen interpretation without automatically justifying inclusion. The project prints the externally controlled short core and preserves the internally coherent but less externally controlled saying in the appendix.
