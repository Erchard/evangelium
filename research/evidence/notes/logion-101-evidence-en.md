# Logion 101 Evidence Rationale v0.1

## Decision

Current publication decision: `DEFER_TO_CLUSTER`.

Reader implication: do not include Logion 101 as a full saying in the clean reconstruction.

## Source Basis

- Coptic base: extant in NHC II,2, with damaged/restored portions in the local working text.
- P.Oxy. status: no loaded Oxyrhynchus Greek witness.
- Greek policy: hypothetical retroversion; family-renunciation core has stronger canonical control than the true-mother/life ending.
- Canonical controls: Luke 14:26 and Matthew 10:37.
- Internal control: Logion 55.

## Rationale

The family-renunciation core is strongly controlled and may belong to early discipleship tradition. The full Thomas form, however, adds a symbolic contrast between bodily mother and true mother who gives life. That ending is less securely controlled and likely belongs to Thomas's symbolic anthropology.

The logion should therefore be deferred to a family-renunciation split-unit cluster rather than included whole.

## Appendix Use

Use this logion to show how an early-looking discipleship core can be embedded in a later or more symbolic Thomas expansion.

## Next Action

Split the family-renunciation core from the true-mother/life ending and compare with Logia 55, Luke 14:26, and Matthew 10:37.
