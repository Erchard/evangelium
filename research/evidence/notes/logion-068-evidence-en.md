## Source Basis

- Coptic base: extant in NHC II,2.
- P.Oxy. status: no loaded Oxyrhynchus Greek witness.
- Greek policy: any Greek form is hypothetical.
- Canonical controls: Matthew 5:10-12 and Luke 6:22-23 for the persecution beatitude.
- Internal control: Logion 69 for the paired persecution beatitude cluster.

## Rationale

The persecution beatitude is strongly controlled by Matthew and Luke, but the Thomas form includes a difficult clause about finding no place where persecution happened. That clause may be Thomasine, eschatological, or textually opaque.

The core is important, yet this pass should not promote it without a beatitude-cluster control note.
