## Main Evidence

Thomas 79 combines two Lukan-like units:

1. a woman blesses the womb and breasts that bore/nursed Jesus, and Jesus redirects blessing to those who hear and keep the Father's word;
2. an eschatological saying blessing wombs that did not conceive and breasts that did not nurse.

The first unit is closely controlled by Luke 11:27-28. The second resembles Luke 23:29.

## Early-Core Assessment

Possible early core:

> Blessed are those who hear the word and keep it.

The womb/breast acclamation and response may be early or shared, but the combination with the later woe/eschatological motif is composite.

## Secondary-Layer Indicators

The full Thomas sequence joins two distinct Lukan-like contexts. That may be Thomasine collection, secondary conflation, or dependence on Luke-like material.

## Limits

No extant Greek Thomas witness is loaded. Direction of dependence is unresolved.
