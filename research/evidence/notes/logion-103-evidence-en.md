## Main Evidence

Thomas 103 is a watchfulness beatitude built around knowing when thieves will come. Matthew and Luke preserve a closely related householder/thief warning. Thomas 21 also contains thief/watchfulness material.

Thomas's form is more elaborate: the person knows the watch, rises, gathers his kingdom/domain, and girds himself before the thieves enter. This may be a Thomasine transformation of a simpler watchfulness core.

## Early-Core Assessment

Included marked reader core:

> Blessed is the one who knows when the thief is coming and keeps watch before he comes.

The thief/watchfulness image is strong. The "gather his kingdom/domain" clause is not needed for the core.

## Secondary-Layer Indicators

The "gather his kingdom/domain" wording is difficult and likely interpretive. The beatitude form may be an adaptation of a warning saying. The internal overlap with Logion 21 means this should be treated as part of a watchfulness cluster.

## Limits

No extant Greek Thomas witness is loaded. The synoptic controls are strong but do not prove independence. The Coptic wording is partly damaged and interpretively difficult.
