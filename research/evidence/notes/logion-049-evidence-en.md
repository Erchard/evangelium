# Logion 49 Evidence Rationale v0.1

## Decision

Current publication decision: `DEFER_TO_CLUSTER`.

Reader implication: do not include Logion 49 in the clean reconstruction as a standalone early saying.

## Source Basis

- Coptic base: extant in NHC II,2.
- P.Oxy. status: no loaded Oxyrhynchus Greek witness for this logion.
- Greek policy: no extant Thomas Greek text; any Greek form is hypothetical.
- Internal controls: Logia 16, 22, 23, and 75 for `monachos`, election, unity, and solitary identity.
- Cluster support: the Thomas unity/monachos cluster is the primary local control.

## Rationale

Logion 49 is central for understanding Thomas, but that does not make it central for the earliest reconstruction. The language of the solitary/elect and return to the kingdom sounds like a developed Thomas identity formula.

Without external controls or an extant Greek witness, it is not suitable for the clean reader. It should remain in the appendix and be interpreted within the unity/monachos cluster.

## Appendix Use

Use the logion to explain the Thomas community's self-understanding: solitary/elect identity, origin from the kingdom, and return. This is valuable for readers even if it is not part of the reconstructed earliest gospel.
