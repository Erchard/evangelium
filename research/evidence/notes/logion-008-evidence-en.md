# Logion 8 Evidence Note

Status: evidence note v0.1.

## Decision

`UNCERTAIN`

## Working Candidate

> A wise fisher chose one large good fish from many small fish.

## Main Reason

Logion 8 is a compact parable and may preserve an independent traditional unit. It has a thematic control in Matthew 13:47-48, where the kingdom is compared to a net thrown into the sea that gathers fish of every kind and is then sorted.

The problem is that the structure is not close enough for reader inclusion:

- Matthew's parable is about the kingdom and final sorting of good and bad fish.
- Thomas 8 is about the human being or wise fisher choosing one large good fish among many small fish.
- Matthew has good/bad sorting; Thomas has large/small selection.
- Thomas ends with a hearing formula.

No loaded Oxyrhynchus Greek Thomas witness preserves Logion 8. A Greek retroversion is possible but low-medium confidence and should not be treated as an extant witness.

## Reconstruction Judgment

Logion 8 should remain in the apparatus as a high-interest parable, but it should not enter the Ukrainian reader yet. It needs broader parable comparison and possibly a study of one/many, large/small, and election motifs inside Thomas.

## Reconstruction Implication

Do not add Logion 8 to the reader text at this stage.
