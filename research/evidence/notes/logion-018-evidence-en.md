## Rationale

Logion 18 contains a potentially early formula, "will not taste death", but its full form is framed by a Thomas-style dialogue about beginning and end. The canonical controls preserve a related phrase in a different eschatological setting, while Thomas reorients the idea toward discovering the beginning and knowing the end.

Because the logion's early-looking phrase is embedded in a developed interpretive frame, it should not be promoted as a clean early unit. It belongs in the appendix as a meaningful but uncertain case.
