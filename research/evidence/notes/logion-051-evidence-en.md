## Decision

Current publication decision: `DEFER_TO_CLUSTER`.

## Source Basis

- Coptic base: extant in NHC II,2.
- P.Oxy. status: no loaded Oxyrhynchus Greek witness for this logion.
- Greek policy: no extant Thomas Greek witness; any Greek form must be a marked retroversion.
- Canonical control: Luke 17:20-21 for the already-present kingdom motif.
- Internal controls: Logia 3 and 113 for hidden or already-present kingdom/world language.

## Rationale

Logion 51 is important for Thomas's realized eschatology: the expected rest/new world has already come, but the disciples do not know it. That theme overlaps with early kingdom material, but the question about the rest of the dead and the new world gives the unit a developed theological frame.

The logion should be studied with Thomas 3 and 113 before any reader decision changes. In this pass, it is not a clean early unit; it is deferred to a realized-eschatology cluster.

## Appendix Use

Use the logion to explain realized eschatology for readers: the difference between future expectation and hidden present reality. The appendix should show both the Luke 17:20-21 thematic control and the internal Thomas parallels.
