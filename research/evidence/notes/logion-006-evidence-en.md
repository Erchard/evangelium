# Logion 6 Evidence Note

Status: evidence note v0.2.

## Decision

Full Logion 6: `UNCERTAIN`

Short ethical core only: `INCLUDE_WITH_MARKER`

## Included Core

English:

> Do not lie, and do not do what you hate.

Ukrainian:

> Не брешіть і не робіть того, що ненавидите.

This is the only part of Logion 6 that should be added to the reader text, and only with a marker.

## Excluded Material

Do not include the full Logion 6 composition at this stage:

- the question about fasting, prayer, almsgiving, and food;
- the hidden/revealed ending.

The hidden/revealed motif is already handled through Logion 5. The ritual question belongs to the wider ritual-ethics cluster with Logia 14, 27, and 104.

## Coptic Witness

Source: `sources/primary_texts/coptic/logia-001-007-coptic-working.md`.

The Coptic witness preserves the full composition: a question about fasting, prayer, almsgiving, and food, followed by the short ethical answer and a hidden/revealed saying.

## Greek Witness

Source: `sources/primary_texts/greek_poxy/dclp-poxy654-62840.xml`.

P.Oxy. 654 is important but lacunose at the core. DCLP reconstructs:

> μὴ ψεύδεσθε καὶ ὃτι μισεῖτε μὴ ποιεῖτε

This supports the presence of the core in the Greek Thomas tradition, but the marker remains necessary because much of the phrase is supplied in lost text.

## Ethical-Core Controls

Detailed table: `controls/synoptic-parallels/logion-006-ethical-core.md`.

The strongest control for the second half of the core is Tobit 4:15:

> καὶ ὃ μισεῖς, μηδενὶ ποιήσῃς

This is a close Jewish sapiential parallel to "do not do what you hate."

Other controls:

- Leviticus 19:11 for the prohibition of lying.
- Zechariah 8:16-17 for truthfulness, false oath, and hated conduct.
- Matthew 7:12 and Luke 6:31 for the positive Golden Rule form.
- Didache 1:2 for early Christian negative reciprocity.
- Colossians 3:9 for direct early Christian "do not lie" language.

## Rationale

The ethical core has a stronger reconstruction profile than the complete logion:

- It is short and memorable.
- It is ethically direct.
- It lacks distinctively Thomasine mythological or unity language.
- It has strong Jewish sapiential support.
- It has early Christian reciprocity controls.
- It is embedded in a Greek Oxyrhynchus witness, though partly reconstructed.

Reasons for caution:

- P.Oxy. 654 is lacunose at the key phrase.
- The extant Coptic unit embeds the core inside a complex ritual-practice composition.
- The hidden/revealed ending may be a reuse of material already represented in Logion 5.

## Probability Assessment

This assessment applies to the short ethical core only:

| Period | Probability | Reason |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Before 60 CE | 25% | The negative Golden Rule has a strong Jewish sapiential background and could predate the Thomas composition. |
| 60-90 CE | 40% | Strong fit for early Jewish-Christian ethical transmission and sayings collection activity. |
| 90-130 CE | 25% | Possible period for integration into the Thomas ritual-ethics unit. |
| After 130 CE | 10% | Less likely for the core itself, though later shaping of the full logion remains possible. |

The full Logion 6 composition retains the earlier cautious profile and remains `UNCERTAIN`.

## Reconstruction Implication

Add only the short ethical core to the Ukrainian reader text with a marker:

Do not add the ritual question or hidden/revealed ending.
