# Logion 9 Evidence Note

Status: working evidence note v0.2.

## Decision

Short sower core: `INCLUDE_WITH_MARKER`.

Full Thomas 9: `UNCERTAIN`.

The short non-allegorical parable core may now be printed in the main reconstructed reader text with a visible marker. The full Coptic form remains in the apparatus.

## Possible Early Core

English:

> A sower went out to sow. Some seed fell on the road, some on rock, some among thorns, and some on good earth; only the good earth bore fruit.

Ukrainian:

> Вийшов сіяч сіяти. Одне насіння впало на дорогу, інше на камінь, інше між терня, інше на добру землю; тільки добра земля дала плід.

This core is included in the main reconstructed text with a marker.

## Coptic Witness

Source: `sources/primary_texts/coptic/linssen-thomas-coptic-unicode-working.txt`.

The Coptic witness preserves the parable itself, without the allegorical interpretation found after the synoptic versions.

Distinctive Thomas features include:

- the sower filling his hand and casting;
- birds gathering the road seed;
- seed on rock failing to root and failing to put up an ear;
- the worm eating the seed among thorns;
- the good earth producing "sixty" and "one hundred twenty."

## Greek Witness

No extant Greek witness for Thomas 9 is present in the loaded Oxyrhynchus witnesses:

- P.Oxy. 654: prologue and Logia 1-7;
- P.Oxy. 1: Logia 26-30, 77b, 31-33;
- P.Oxy. 655: Logia 24(?), 36-39.

This is a significant limitation. Any Greek form for Thomas 9 must be labeled `Greek retroversion, hypothetical`.

## Synoptic Parallels

Synoptic control table: `controls/synoptic-parallels/logion-009-sower.md`.

The closest controls are:

- Mark 4:3-9;
- Matthew 13:3-9;
- Luke 8:5-8.

All three preserve a sower parable with road/path, birds, rocky ground, thorns, good earth, and fruit. Mark and Matthew also include a yield series; Luke has a simpler hundredfold yield. Thomas differs from all three by preserving `60 / 120`.

The synoptic allegorical interpretations are also important controls:

- Mark 4:13-20;
- Matthew 13:18-23;
- Luke 8:11-15.

These passages identify the seed with the word, the soils with different hearers, and the failures with Satan, persecution, cares, wealth, desire, or lack of endurance. Thomas 9 does not preserve this interpretive layer. That absence does not prove Thomasine priority, but it supports printing only the short parable core rather than importing the later allegorical framework into the reconstruction.

## Rationale

Reasons for importance:

- The parable is widely attested in synoptic tradition.
- Thomas preserves the parable without the allegorical interpretation.
- The lack of allegorical interpretation could point toward an earlier parable form.
- Thomas has distinctive details that do not look like straightforward copying of one synoptic text.

Reasons for caution about the full Thomas form:

- There is no loaded Greek witness for Thomas 9.
- The direction of dependence is unresolved.
- The absence of allegory may reflect an earlier form, but it may also reflect Thomasine selection or abbreviation.
- The distinctive details, especially the worm and `60 / 120`, require further study.

## Probability Assessment

| Period | Probability | Reason |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Before 60 CE | 20% | A compact agrarian parable could preserve early oral tradition. |
| 60-90 CE | 30% | A shared mobile parable tradition alongside early synoptic streams is plausible. |
| 90-130 CE | 35% | The extant Thomas form may reflect Thomasine or post-synoptic reshaping of an older parable. |
| After 130 CE | 15% | Possible for final shaping of the Coptic-preserved form; less likely for the whole parable core. |

## Reconstruction Implication

Add only the short sower core to `reconstructed-gospel-uk.md` with a marker.

Do not add the worm, the `60 / 120` yield, the "fruit upward to heaven" phrase, or the synoptic allegorical interpretations to the main reader text. These remain apparatus material pending deeper analysis.
