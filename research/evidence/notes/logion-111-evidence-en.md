# Logion 111 Evidence Rationale v0.1

## Decision

Current publication decision: `DEFER_TO_CLUSTER`.

Reader implication: do not include Logion 111 as a full saying in the clean reconstruction.

## Source Basis

- Coptic base: extant in NHC II,2.
- P.Oxy. status: no loaded Oxyrhynchus Greek witness.
- Greek policy: hypothetical retroversion; the heaven/earth passing subunit has stronger canonical control than the full composition.
- Canonical controls: Mark 13:31, Matthew 24:35, and Luke 21:33 for heaven and earth passing.
- Internal controls: Logia 1, 18, 19 for not seeing/tasting death; Logia 56, 80, and 110 for world-unworthy language.

## Rationale

Logion 111 is composite. It combines heaven/earth passing, living-from-the-Living and not seeing death, and a world-unworthy self-finding saying. The first subunit has strong synoptic control, but the full combination is Thomasine and requires splitting.

It is too important to leave unexplained, but not suitable for clean-reader inclusion as a single unit.

## Appendix Use

Use Logion 111 as a major cluster case: eschatology, deathlessness, self-finding, and world-unworthiness converge here.

## Next Action

Split the heaven/earth passing core from the living/death and world-unworthy material; study with Logia 18, 56, and 80.
