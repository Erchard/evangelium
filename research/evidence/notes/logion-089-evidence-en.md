## Main Evidence

Thomas 89 has a short inside/outside cup saying. Luke is the closest control because it includes the argument from the maker of the inside and outside. Matthew shares the cup and inside/outside motif but embeds it in a stronger anti-Pharisaic polemic.

Thomas lacks an explicit Pharisee frame and presents the saying as a general criticism of external washing without inner understanding. This could be a shorter independent form, but it could also be a secondary abstraction from Luke.

## Early-Core Assessment

Possible early core:

> Why wash the outside of the cup? The one who made the inside also made the outside.

The short form is coherent and can function without the larger Matthean/Lukan polemical setting.

## Secondary-Layer Indicators

The close overlap with Luke 11:40 creates a real dependence warning. The saying also fits later inner/outer spiritual interpretation, which Thomas may intensify elsewhere.

## Limits

No extant Greek Thomas witness is loaded. The Greek layer, if reconstructed, is hypothetical. The absence of the Pharisee frame may be primitive brevity or secondary decontextualization.
