# Logion 38 Evidence Note

Status: evidence rationale v0.1.

## Decision

`UNCERTAIN`; keep outside the clean reader.

## Working Unit

> You desired to hear these words; days will come when you will seek me and not find me.

## Textual Witnesses

- Coptic base: extant NHC II,2; preserves the full saying.
- P.Oxy. 1: not preserved.
- P.Oxy. 654: not preserved.
- P.Oxy. 655: extant but fragmentary and heavily restored.
- Canonical controls: Matthew 13:17, Luke 10:24, Luke 17:22, and Johannine seek/not-find motifs are controls only.

## Main Evidence

P.Oxy. 655 likely witnesses the saying in Greek, but the continuous wording depends heavily on editorial restoration. The saying also has a collection-authorizing character: it emphasizes the privilege of hearing "these words" and the coming absence of Jesus.

## Reason For Non-Inclusion

The fragmentary Greek support is not enough to overcome the post-Easter or collection-frame profile of the saying. It may preserve an early absence motif, but not yet a clean early saying for the reconstructed reader.

## Reconstruction Implication

Keep Logion 38 in the appendix. Do not use the restored Greek as if it were a secure continuous manuscript text.

## Remaining Work

Reassess after the stronger P.Oxy. 655 material in Logia 36 and 39 has been fully polished. A future pass should distinguish the hearing-privilege unit from the seek/not-find unit.
