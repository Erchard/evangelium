# Logion 77 Evidence Note

Status: evidence rationale v0.1.

## Decision

`DEFER`; keep outside the clean reader.

## Working Unit

> Full Coptic Logion 77 combines light/all christology with the stone/wood presence unit.

## Textual Witnesses

- Coptic base: extant NHC II,2; preserves the full light/all plus stone/wood form.
- P.Oxy. 1: extant overlap for the 77b stone/wood unit only, immediately after the damaged Logion 30 material.
- P.Oxy. 654: not preserved.
- P.Oxy. 655: not preserved.
- Canonical controls: Johannine "light" language is thematic only, not a Thomas witness.

## Main Evidence

P.Oxy. 1 gives direct Greek support for the stone/wood presence unit:

> Lift the stone and there you will find me; split the wood and I am there.

It does not attest the full Coptic "I am the light / I am the All" opening. The Greek manuscript sequence also connects this unit with Logion 30 material rather than with the full Coptic Logion 77 arrangement.

## Reason For Non-Inclusion

The full logion is too theologically expansive for unmarked inclusion. The Greek-supported 77b unit is promising, but its textual placement and relation to Logion 30 must be studied before it can be isolated as a reader core.

## Reconstruction Implication

Do not print the full Logion 77 in the clean reader. Treat 77b as a high-priority split-core candidate.

## Remaining Work

Create a Logion 30 / 77b control note. Evaluate whether 77b can stand as an independent early presence saying while 77a remains appendix-only.
