# Logion 52 Evidence Rationale v0.1

## Decision

Current publication decision: `DEFER_TO_CLUSTER`.

Reader implication: do not include Logion 52 in the clean reconstruction as a standalone saying.

## Source Basis

- Coptic base: extant in NHC II,2.
- P.Oxy. status: no loaded Oxyrhynchus Greek witness for this logion.
- Greek policy: any Greek form must be marked as hypothetical.
- Internal controls: Logia 11, 59, and 111 for living/dead language.
- Canonical/thematic control: Luke 24:27, 44 for prophetic witness to Jesus, but not as a direct Thomas wording control.

## Rationale

The saying is theologically important but frame-like. The disciples interpret Jesus through the twenty-four prophets, and Jesus redirects them from the dead to the Living One before them. That makes the logion useful for Thomas's view of revelation and presence, but not secure as an early independent sayings unit.

Because it lacks a Greek Thomas witness and depends mainly on internal Thomas themes, it should be deferred to the living/dead and prophetic-witness cluster.

## Appendix Use

Use this logion to explain how Thomas contrasts inherited prophetic discourse with present recognition of the Living One.
