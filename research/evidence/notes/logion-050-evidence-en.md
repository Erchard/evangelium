# Logion 50 Evidence Rationale v0.1

## Decision

Current publication decision: `DEFER_TO_CLUSTER`.

Reader implication: do not include Logion 50 in the clean reconstruction as a full unit.

## Source Basis

- Coptic base: extant in NHC II,2, with some damaged/restored places in the line.
- P.Oxy. status: no loaded Oxyrhynchus Greek witness for this logion.
- Greek policy: any Greek layer is hypothetical; do not present it as extant manuscript evidence.
- Internal controls: Logia 24, 49, and 83 for light, image, origin, and election language.
- Canonical controls: none currently transcribed in the local control files for this logion.

## Rationale

Logion 50 is a catechetical identity dialogue: where the disciples come from, who they are, and what sign of the Father is in them. Its language of light, self-originating light, image, children/elect, movement, and rest is theologically dense and characteristically Thomasine.

It is valuable for reconstructing Thomas theology, but not secure enough for reconstructing the earliest gospel-like layer. The saying should be deferred to a light/election cluster.

## Appendix Use

Use Logion 50 as a major interpretive appendix entry. It should help readers understand Thomas's identity language while clearly distinguishing that interpretive value from clean-reader inclusion.
