## Main Evidence

Thomas 72 and Luke 12 share a distinctive inheritance-dispute scene:

- a man asks Jesus to intervene in a family inheritance matter;
- Jesus refuses the role of divider/judge;
- the refusal turns attention away from property arbitration.

Thomas has a compact dialogue ending with the question whether Jesus is a divider.

## Early-Core Assessment

Possible early core:

> A man asked Jesus to divide an inheritance; Jesus refused to be a divider.

This is a plausible early anecdotal saying because it is short, socially concrete, and not dependent on developed church doctrine.

## Secondary-Layer Indicators

The Thomas form may be an extracted or shortened version of Luke's scene. Conversely, Luke may expand a shorter refusal saying with an anti-greed application. Direction is unresolved.

## Limits

Only one major synoptic control. No loaded extant Greek Thomas witness.
