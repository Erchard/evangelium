## Main Evidence

Thomas 107 preserves the lost-sheep parable: a shepherd has one hundred sheep, one is lost, he leaves the ninety-nine and searches until he finds the one. Matthew and Luke strongly control this parable.

Thomas has distinctive features: the lost sheep is "the largest," and the shepherd says, "I love you more than the ninety-nine." These features are not present in the synoptic versions and may reflect Thomasine valuation of the unique one.

## Early-Core Assessment

Possible early core:

> A shepherd with one hundred sheep loses one, leaves the ninety-nine, and searches until he finds it.

This core is plausible as an early parable. The "largest sheep" and love-more-than-ninety-nine elements should remain marked.

## Secondary-Layer Indicators

The "largest" sheep may reshape the parable from rescue of the lost into election of the superior or singular one. The love statement may intensify the one-over-many motif, which resonates with Thomasine collectional patterns.

## Limits

No extant Greek Thomas witness is loaded. The parable is strongly controlled by Matthew and Luke, but dependence direction remains unresolved. Thomas's distinctive ending may be secondary.
