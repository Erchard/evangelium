# Logion 26 Evidence Note

Status: evidence note v0.1.

## Decision

`INCLUDE_WITH_MARKER`

Logion 26 may be added to the Ukrainian reader text with a visible marker.

## Working Early Core

Possible early core:

> First remove the beam from your own eye; then you will see clearly to remove the speck from your brother's eye.

## Main Reason

Logion 26 has a strong evidence profile:

- Coptic Thomas preserves the complete saying.
- P.Oxy. 1 preserves the final part in Greek.
- Matthew 7:3-5 and Luke 6:41-42 preserve very close synoptic parallels.
- The saying is short, ethical, memorable, and not dependent on distinctively Thomasine mythological language.

It still requires a marker because the Greek Thomas witness is fragmentary and the direction of dependence remains unresolved.

## Greek Witness

P.Oxy. 1 preserves:

> καὶ τότε διαβλέψεις / ἐκβαλεῖν τὸ κάρφος / τὸ ἐν τῷ ὀφθαλμῷ / τοῦ ἀδελφοῦ σου

This corresponds to the final clause of Thomas 26: "then you will see clearly to remove the speck from your brother's eye."

The Greek witness is important, but partial. It confirms the presence of the saying in an early Greek Thomas witness but does not preserve the opening contrast between the speck and the beam.

## Synoptic Comparison

Detailed table: `controls/synoptic-parallels/logion-026-speck-beam.md`.

Matthew and Luke have a more rhetorically expanded form with questions and "hypocrite." Thomas is shorter and more direct. This may indicate an earlier compact form, or it may be an abbreviation. The evidence does not decide the direction.
