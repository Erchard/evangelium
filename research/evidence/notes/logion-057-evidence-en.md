Status: working evidence note v0.1.

## Decision

`UNCERTAIN`; possible parable candidate, but dependence on Matthew remains a major warning.

Confidence: low-medium.

## Textual Witnesses

- NHC II,2, local working text.

Greek Thomas witness:

- No loaded Greek Oxyrhynchus Thomas witness preserves Logion 57.
- Any Greek form must be labeled `Greek retroversion, hypothetical`.

Synoptic control:

- Matthew 13:24-30, with Matthew 13:36-43 as interpretive control.

## Main Evidence

Thomas 57 is very close to the Matthean parable of weeds among wheat. Both share good seed, an enemy sowing weeds, refusal to uproot early, harvest, separation, and burning.

Thomas lacks Matthew's explicit allegorical interpretation. That may suggest a less allegorized form, but it may also be an abbreviated or extracted Matthean form.

## Early-Core Assessment

Possible early core:

> An enemy sowed weeds among good seed; the owner waited until harvest, lest the wheat be uprooted with the weeds.

This parable core is coherent, but its independence is not established.

## Secondary-Layer Indicators

The parable is known only through Matthew among the synoptics. The closeness to Matthew creates a serious dependence risk.

## Limits

No extant Greek Thomas witness is loaded. Without stronger external controls, this should not enter the clean reconstruction.
