# Logion 30 Evidence Note

Status: evidence rationale v0.1.

## Decision

`UNCERTAIN`; keep outside the clean reader.

## Working Unit

> A damaged saying about three, one, and Jesus' presence with the one/two; textually connected in P.Oxy. 1 with the 77b stone/wood unit.

## Textual Witnesses

- Coptic base: extant NHC II,2; preserves a difficult form about three gods and two or one.
- P.Oxy. 1: extant but damaged and textually complex; preserves a Greek presence unit and then continues into material corresponding to Coptic Thomas 77b.
- P.Oxy. 654: not preserved.
- P.Oxy. 655: not preserved.
- Canonical control: Matthew 18:20 is a strong thematic control for presence with two/three, but not a Thomas witness.
- Cluster control: unity/monachos/presence cluster.

## Main Evidence

P.Oxy. 1 is valuable because it shows Greek circulation of this material. It is also the main reason for caution: the manuscript sequence blurs the boundary between Logion 30 and 77b, and the Greek and Coptic forms do not map cleanly onto one another.

## Reason For Non-Inclusion

The saying cannot yet be responsibly printed as a clean early unit. The most promising element may be the presence-with-one/two core, but the textual state and relation to Matthew 18:20 require a separate control pass.

## Reconstruction Implication

Do not include Logion 30 in the clean reader. Treat it as a high-priority split-core candidate in the appendix.

## Remaining Work

Create a dedicated Logion 30 / 77b P.Oxy. 1 control file. Test whether the presence unit and stone/wood unit should be evaluated as separate recoverable cores.
