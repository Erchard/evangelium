# Greek Reconstruction Coverage Audit - 2026-07-19

## Purpose

This audit records the current state of the Greek layer after the print-facing Ukrainian appendix was corrected. Reader-facing text must not say that the Greek text is simply absent when a Greek form can and should be reconstructed from the Coptic witness. Where no direct Greek Thomas papyrus survives, the appendix should use the concise label: **Реконструйований грецький текст**.

## Editorial Rule Going Forward

For every logion, the card and the paper appendix should present the Greek layer in one of two reader-facing forms:

1. Direct papyrus witness, with a short source label such as `P.Oxy. 654`.
2. `Реконструйований грецький текст`, followed by the reconstructed Greek line.

Do not use technical phrases such as "loaded witnesses", "not extant in loaded corpus", or long explanations inside the Greek text block. Longer cautions belong in the reconstruction rationale, not in the source-text slot.
