# Commons Dedication and Use Policy

Status: project policy v0.1, 2026-07-18.

## Core Principle

The EUAGELIA project is not intended to become the private intellectual property of one person, organization, publisher, church, academic institution, or company. The reconstructed text, commentary framework, source apparatus, prompts, and research architecture are offered as work for the common human good.

The guiding idea:

> This work belongs to humanity. Anyone may use it, including commercially, provided they do not convert it into exclusive private ownership or a copyright monopoly.

## What May Be Done

Anyone may:

- read, copy, and share the project;
- print the reconstructed text or commentary;
- translate it into other languages;
- quote it in books, articles, websites, courses, videos, or public discussions;
- adapt it for study editions, devotional editions, academic editions, digital tools, datasets, or teaching materials;
- use it commercially.

Commercial use is explicitly allowed because the goal is not to prevent circulation, printing, teaching, or publishing. A printed book, paid course, commercial app, or documentary may use the project if it respects the anti-ownership condition.

## What Must Not Be Done

No user may present the underlying EUAGELIA reconstruction or project framework as their exclusive property.

Users must not:

- claim that they alone own the reconstructed text as such;
- claim copyright monopoly over the project materials created here;
- prevent others from using the same underlying reconstruction;
- put the shared project behind a legal claim that blocks further public use;
- imply that the project belongs exclusively to one person, company, church, publisher, or institution.

Derivative works may have their own added material, design, commentary, layout, or publishing labor. But the underlying reconstruction, project method, and shared evidence framework should remain available as common human heritage.

## Attribution and Transparency

Attribution is encouraged because it helps readers verify sources and follow the history of the reconstruction. But attribution is not a tool of ownership.

Suggested print-safe citation:

> EUAGELIA Project, *A Reconstruction of the Earliest Sayings Gospel*, working repository, 2026.

Suggested Ukrainian citation:

> Проєкт EUAGELIA, *Реконструкція найдавнішого Євангелія висловів*, робочий репозиторій, 2026.

## Relation to Source Rights

This policy applies only to original project contributions:

- our reconstruction decisions;
- original Ukrainian and English translations created in the project;
- original commentary and explanations;
- original evidence notes, tables, audits, prompts, and methodology documents;
- original digital/print architecture.

It does not change the rights status of external materials:

- manuscript facsimiles;
- protected academic editions;
- licensed databases;
- modern copyrighted translations;
- external photographs or images;
- third-party source transcriptions not released under compatible terms.

Those materials must keep their own source labels, citation notes, and rights restrictions. The project may use protected materials for verification or short citation where lawful, but not as a public-domain base text unless their rights status allows it.

## Implementation Rule for Final Products

Every final Ukrainian book, English book, and digital scholarly companion should include a short public notice based on this policy.

Recommended short notice:

> The original EUAGELIA reconstruction, translations, commentary, and research framework are offered as common human heritage. Anyone may use, adapt, publish, or commercially use them, provided they do not claim exclusive ownership or restrict others from using the same shared materials. Third-party sources retain their own rights status.

## Legal Precision Still Needed

This document states the project's ethical and publication intent. Before formal public release, the project should decide whether to express this in a standard legal instrument or in a custom publication notice.

The current best-fit legal direction is a public-domain-style dedication plus an explicit anti-appropriation notice. However, a pure public-domain dedication such as CC0 may not enforce the anti-ownership condition. If enforceability matters, a qualified rights specialist should review the final language.
