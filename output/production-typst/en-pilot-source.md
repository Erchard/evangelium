## Logion 114
### Reconstruction Status
- Main text: no.
- Greek witness status: No loaded P.Oxy. witness.
### What This Saying Is About
This final scene is the clearest exclusion case. It stages Mary, Peter, and gender transformation in a way that looks like later community-boundary discourse rather than an early sayings-gospel unit.
### Text In This Edition
No main-text text is printed for this logion in the current English reconstruction. The logion remains visible here so that readers can see the evidence, the interpretive value, and the reason why it was not allowed into the main reconstructed text.
### Sources And Controls
Primary source/control summary: evidence note plus publication exclusion rationale plus control package.
For this edition the complete Coptic witness carries the main textual burden; no loaded Oxyrhynchus Greek witness is available for this logion. Coptic NHC II,2 remains the complete manuscript base for the received Thomas text; P.Oxy. 1, P.Oxy. 654, and P.Oxy. 655 are used where this project's loaded evidence supplies extant or partial Greek control. Canonical Greek parallels are controls, not Thomas witnesses.
### Why It Is Or Is Not In The Reconstruction
This edition excludes this logion from the clean reconstruction because it most likely belongs to a later interpretive or community-shaping layer. It is still discussed fully because exclusion must be argued, not hidden. Late/secondary Mary/Peter/gender-transformation frame; does not fit earliest sayings-gospel reconstruction under current method. The appendix therefore uses this logion as a transparent case study in exclusion.
### Possible Interpretations
1. Early-core reading: the full logion may preserve a compact saying or motif that circulated before later literary framing.
2. Wisdom reading: the saying may work as a compact challenge that forces the reader to rethink an ordinary assumption.
3. Editorial reading: the received Thomas form may have expanded, combined, or reframed an older unit in a way that now requires caution.
4. Appendix-only reading: the logion can still illuminate the tradition even when it is not printed as part of the reconstructed main text.
5. Source-caution reading: without a loaded Oxyrhynchus Greek witness, the Coptic witness carries the main textual weight for this edition.
