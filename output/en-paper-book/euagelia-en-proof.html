Logion 1

Whoever finds the interpretation of these words will not taste death.

Logion 2

Let the one who seeks not stop seeking until they find.

When they find, they will be disturbed; when disturbed, they will be amazed; and they will reign.

Logion 3

Do not seek the kingdom as a place in heaven or in the sea: the kingdom is within you and outside you.

When you know yourselves, you will be known, and you will know that you are children of the living Father. If you do not know yourselves, you are in poverty, and you are poverty.

Logion 4

Know what is before your face, and what is hidden from you will be revealed to you.

Logion 6

Do not lie, and do not do what you hate.

Logion 10

Logion 16

Logion 20

Logion 22

Logion 25

Logion 26

Logion 31

A prophet is not accepted in his own place.

And a physician does not heal those who know him.

Logion 32

A city built and fortified on a high mountain cannot fall and cannot be hidden.

Logion 33

Logion 34

If a blind person leads a blind person, both will fall into a pit.

Logion 35

Logion 36

Logion 39

Logion 41

Logion 45

Logion 46

How To Read The Appendix

The logion numbers follow the conventional numbering of the Gospel of Thomas. Where only a core or subunit is printed in the clean reconstruction, the paper book keeps the familiar logion number for lookup, while the appendix explains the partial-inclusion decision.

Witness labels: NHC II,2 is the Coptic manuscript witness; P.Oxy. 1, P.Oxy. 654, and P.Oxy. 655 are Greek papyrus witnesses; Greek retroversion, hypothetical is a modern controlled reconstruction of a plausible Greek form, not an extant manuscript witness.

Methodological Summary

This reconstruction is not a translation of the whole Coptic Gospel of Thomas. It is a selective reconstruction of the earliest recoverable layer of sayings tradition. The clean reader prints only the units judged responsible for this edition. Excluded, deferred, and uncertain logia remain visible in the appendix so that readers can evaluate the reconstruction rather than merely receive it.
